# North American Public Avalanche Danger Scale - Reference Card

| Level | Name | Likelihood | Size / Distribution | Travel Advice |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Low | Unlikely | Small avalanches in isolated areas or extreme terrain | Generally safe. Normal caution. |
| 2 | Moderate | Possible on specific features | Small in specific areas; Large in isolated | Heightened conditions on specific terrain features. Evaluate snow and terrain carefully. |
| 3 | Considerable | Likely natural; very likely human | Large in specific; Very large in isolated | Dangerous. Careful snowpack evaluation, cautious route-finding, conservative decision-making. |
| 4 | High | Very likely | Large in many; Very large in specific | Very dangerous. Travel in avalanche terrain NOT recommended. |
| 5 | Extreme | Certain | Very large in many | Avoid all avalanche terrain. |

## Common Drafting Errors

- Publishing Level 3 without matching problem-type likelihood/size.
- Using Level 2 language ("isolated, specific") for a Level 3 distribution.
- Mixing tones: "Considerable" in the rating and "generally stable" in the narrative.

## Confidence Modifiers

Every bulletin should include a confidence statement: High / Moderate / Low, with a one-line justification.

- **High** - multiple independent recent observations, consistent weather pattern, no new loading.
- **Moderate** - some observations, but key aspects/elevations under-sampled, or weather expected to change.
- **Low** - thin data, rapid change expected, or active incident complicating signal.
