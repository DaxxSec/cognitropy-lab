# Avalanche Problem Type Matrix

Adopted from the Conceptual Model of Avalanche Hazard (CMAH) and the North American Avalanche Problem Types, used by CAIC, AAC, NWAC, SAC, Avalanche Canada, and the USFS National Avalanche Center.

| Type | Typical Location | Distribution | Sensitivity | Time Scale | Canonical Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dry Loose | Steep, unconsolidated surface snow | Narrow, predictable | Moderate - high during storm | Hours | Recent sluffing, low-cohesion surface |
| Wet Loose | Sun-exposed, warming aspects | Predictable by solar exposure | High during warming | Hours | Wet point-releases, pinwheels |
| Storm Slab | New snow slab on old interface | Broad, storm-period | Very high during storm + 24 h | 1-2 days | Recent natural slabs, ECTP in storm snow |
| Wind Slab | Leeward ridges, cross-loaded gullies | Localized | High on loaded features | Days | Blown-in snow, wind texture, pillowing |
| Persistent Slab | Slab on buried surface hoar / facets / near-surface facets | Can be broad | Variable; spatially variable | Weeks to months | ECTP sudden planar, remote triggering |
| Deep Persistent Slab | Basal facets / depth hoar | Broad in basin, specific aspects | Low likelihood, high consequence | Months | Deep weak layer, large-scale releases |
| Wet Slab | Slab weakened by water percolation | Rapidly changing | Very high when triggered | Hours to days | Glide-like behavior, surface water, isothermal columns |
| Cornice Fall | Ridge-top cornices | Localized to cornice features | Variable; temperature-driven | Days | Cornice cracks, past falls |
| Glide | Slab on smooth ground (grass, rock) | Localized, recurring | Unpredictable timing | Weeks | Glide cracks opening over time |

## Likelihood / Size Decision Rules of Thumb

- A single ECTP <= 13 sudden planar on a representative slope = at least Possible likelihood for Persistent Slab.
- Natural avalanche activity in the last 24 h on a problem type = Likely likelihood.
- Spatial distribution on more than 60% of target aspects = Broad.
- Historical Deep Persistent Slab releases typically D3 or larger - size biases up even at low likelihood.

## When NOT to Add a Problem

- Single isolated pit with no representativeness -> flag as "tracking" not "publishing."
- Cornice observations from months ago without recent growth -> not an active problem.
- Wet loose last week but it froze hard this morning -> not today's problem.
