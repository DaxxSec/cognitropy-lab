# Stability Test Reference

## Compression Test (CT)
- 30 cm x 30 cm isolated column, 1 m deep-ish
- Tap sequence: 10 from wrist, 10 from elbow, 10 from shoulder
- Score: CT# @ depth (failure character)
- Failure character:
  - SP (sudden planar) - pop and slide on a clean plane
  - SC (sudden collapse) - collapse with no planar failure
  - PC (progressive compression) - step-wise
  - RP (resistant planar) - planar but takes effort
  - BRK (break) - irregular

## Extended Column Test (ECT)
- 90 cm across x 30 cm wide isolated column
- Same tap sequence, struck at one end
- Score: ECTP# (propagates full column), ECTN# (initiated but no propagation), ECTX (no initiation)
- ECTP <= 15 sudden -> HIGH concern
- ECTP 16-30 sudden -> MODERATE
- ECTN / ECTX -> combine with other evidence, do not treat as all-clear

## Propagation Saw Test (PST)
- 1 m column along slope, cut a slot in the weak layer
- Saw incrementally, note distance at END (end of block), ARR (arrest), SF (slab fracture)
- Score: PST ##/100 END / ARR / SF @ depth
- < 50/100 END = propagation likely
- > 80/100 ARR = propagation unlikely on this test

## Rutschblock (RB)
- 2 m x 1.5 m isolated block, step-loaded by skier
- Score 1-7
  - 1 = fails during cutting
  - 2 = fails on approach
  - 3-4 = fails with static skier load
  - 5-6 = fails with jumps
  - 7 = no failure

## Interpretation Pooling

- No single test publishes the bulletin. Pool across 3+ representative tests where possible.
- Track **trend** of tests through the storm cycle, not just today's.
- Failure character matters more than tap count for propagation likelihood.
