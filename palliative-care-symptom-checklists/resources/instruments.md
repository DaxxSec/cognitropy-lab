# Instruments — Canonical References

This file documents where each supported instrument comes from and how the deploying team should source an authoritative copy. The agent does not bundle full-text copies of licensed instruments; teams bring their own.

## ESAS-r — Edmonton Symptom Assessment System (revised)
- Origin: Regional Palliative Care Program, Alberta Health Services.
- Use: free for clinical and non-commercial use with attribution.
- Citation: Watanabe SM et al. "A multicenter study comparing two numerical versions of the Edmonton Symptom Assessment System in palliative care patients." *J Pain Symptom Manage.* 2011.
- Source to bring to deployment: the clinic / hospital's own adopted ESAS-r form, or the Alberta Health Services canonical release.

## IPOS — Integrated Palliative care Outcome Scale
- Origin: Cicely Saunders Institute, King's College London.
- Use: free for non-commercial clinical and research use **with registration**.
- Citation: Murtagh FE et al. "A brief, patient- and proxy-reported outcome measure in advanced illness." *Palliat Med.* 2019.
- Source: register at the Cicely Saunders Institute POS website; deploy only with the licensed form in hand.

## POS-S — Palliative care Outcome Scale — Symptoms
- Same institute as IPOS; same registration path.

## Abbey Pain Scale
- Origin: Abbey, J. et al., 2004.
- Use: clinical use with attribution is permitted; check the distributor's latest terms.
- Citation: Abbey J et al. "The Abbey pain scale: a 1-minute numerical indicator for people with end-stage dementia." *Int J Palliat Nurs.* 2004.

## PAINAD — Pain Assessment in Advanced Dementia
- Origin: Warden V, Hurley AC, Volicer L, 2003.
- Use: free for clinical use with attribution.
- Citation: Warden V et al. "Development and psychometric evaluation of the Pain Assessment in Advanced Dementia (PAINAD) scale." *J Am Med Dir Assoc.* 2003.

## RASS — Richmond Agitation-Sedation Scale
- Origin: Sessler CN et al., 2002.
- Use: free for clinical use with attribution.
- Citation: Sessler CN et al. *Am J Respir Crit Care Med.* 2002.

## CAM — Confusion Assessment Method
- Origin: Inouye SK et al., 1990.
- Use: licensed through the CAM training manual; free for some non-commercial uses.
- Citation: Inouye SK et al. "Clarifying confusion: the confusion assessment method." *Ann Intern Med.* 1990.

## Karnofsky Performance Status (KPS)
- Origin: Karnofsky DA, 1948.
- Use: public domain in most jurisdictions.

## Palliative Performance Scale (PPS)
- Origin: Victoria Hospice Society, 1996; PPSv2 most common.
- Use: free for clinical use with attribution.

## FICA Spiritual Assessment Tool
- Origin: Puchalski C, 1999.
- Use: free for clinical use with attribution.
- Citation: Puchalski C, Romer AL. "Taking a spiritual history allows clinicians to understand patients more fully." *J Palliat Med.* 2000.

## Deploying team responsibilities

1. Confirm the licensing terms for each instrument you intend to use.
2. Hold authoritative copies (paper or PDF) of each scale in the team's documentation library.
3. Keep a log of which version / year of each scale is in clinical use.
4. Re-check licensing annually.
