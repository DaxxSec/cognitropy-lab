# Serious-Illness Conversation Prompts

Reference prompts for clinicians preparing a goals-of-care or serious-illness conversation. The agent uses these (and only these) when drafting `/family-meeting-prep` material. The agent does not improvise new conversation prompts.

## Core prompts (Ariadne Labs Serious Illness Conversation Guide tradition)
- What is your understanding now of where you are with your illness?
- How much information about what is likely to be ahead with your illness would you like from me?
- If your health situation worsens, what are your most important goals?
- What are your biggest fears and worries about the future with your health?
- What abilities are so critical to your life that you can't imagine living without them?
- How much are you willing to go through for the possibility of gaining more time?
- How much does your family know about your priorities and wishes?

## Surprise-question prompt (for the clinician only, not the patient)
- Would I be surprised if this patient died in the next year?

## Prompts appropriate for advanced / imminent phase
- What matters most to you now?
- Is there anyone you want to talk to, or anything you want to say?
- What would be a good day, today, look like?

## What the agent should not do
- Do not generate new "clever" conversation prompts.
- Do not suggest what the answer should be.
- Do not suggest what decisions the family should reach.
