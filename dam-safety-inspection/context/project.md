# Project Context

## Objective

Provide an intelligent assistant for dam safety inspection programs that combines regulatory knowledge, engineering domain expertise, and automated data analysis to help inspection teams work more efficiently and catch potential problems earlier.

## Problem Statement

Dam owners and inspection teams face several challenges:

1. **Data volume** — Modern SCADA-connected instrumentation systems generate continuous readings across dozens of sensors. Manual review of this data is time-consuming and error-prone.

2. **Expertise gap** — Many dam owners (particularly small municipal utilities, irrigation districts, and private landowners) lack dedicated dam safety engineering staff.

3. **Regulatory complexity** — Dam safety jurisdiction varies by state and federal oversight agency, each with different reporting requirements and inspection standards.

4. **Aging infrastructure** — The majority of US dams were built between 1950-1980. As they age, monitoring and inspection become more critical, not less.

## Scope

This workspace covers:
- All major dam types (embankment, concrete gravity, arch, buttress, RCC)
- Federal regulatory frameworks (FEMA, FERC, USACE) and general state program alignment
- Standard instrumentation suites (piezometers, inclinometers, settlement monuments, seepage weirs, drain flow, survey)
- Anomaly detection in time-series monitoring data
- Emergency Action Plan review
- Condition assessment and deficiency classification

## Target Users
- Dam safety engineers and inspectors
- Dam owners and operators
- State dam safety program staff
- Consulting engineers performing periodic inspections
- Emergency management personnel involved in dam EAPs
