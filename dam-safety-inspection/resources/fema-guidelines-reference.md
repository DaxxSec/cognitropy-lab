# FEMA Dam Safety Guidelines Reference

## Key FEMA Publications

### FEMA 93 — Federal Guidelines for Dam Safety (2004)
The foundational document for the National Dam Safety Program. Establishes principles for dam safety including hazard classification, inspection requirements, and emergency planning. All federally owned or regulated dams must comply.

### FEMA P-1025 — Federal Guidelines for Dam Safety Risk Management (2015)
Introduces risk-informed decision making to dam safety. Provides framework for estimating probability and consequences of dam failure and using this to prioritize safety investments. Key concepts include:
- Annual failure probability estimation
- Consequence categories (life loss, economic, environmental)
- f-N charts for risk comparison
- ALARP (As Low As Reasonably Practicable) principle

### FEMA 145 — Dam Safety: An Owner's Guidance Manual (2007)
Practical guide for dam owners covering responsibilities, inspection practices, maintenance, and emergency planning. Written at a non-technical level appropriate for small dam owners.

### FEMA 333 — Dam Safety: Hazard Potential Classification System for Dams
Defines the three-tier hazard potential classification (high, significant, low) based on downstream consequences of failure. Classification drives inspection frequency, regulatory oversight level, and emergency planning requirements.

### FEMA 64 — Federal Guidelines for Dam Safety: Emergency Action Planning for Dam Owners (2013)
Comprehensive guide for developing and maintaining Emergency Action Plans. Covers:
- EAP development process
- Required components (notification, detection, evaluation, response)
- Inundation mapping
- Coordination with emergency management
- Annual exercises and plan updates

## FERC Engineering Guidelines

### Part 12 — Safety of Water Power Projects and Project Works
FERC's dam safety program for licensed hydropower dams. Key requirements:
- **Part 12D**: Independent Consultant inspection every 5 years
- **Part 12F**: Owner's Dam Safety Program requirements
- **Supporting Technical Information Documents (STIDs)**: Detailed guidance on specific topics

### FERC Engineering Guidelines Chapters
- Chapter 2: Selecting and Accommodating Inflow Design Floods
- Chapter 3: Gravity Dams
- Chapter 4: Embankment Dams
- Chapter 5: Geotechnical Investigations
- Chapter 9: Dam Instrumentation and Monitoring
- Chapter 11: Arch Dams
- Chapter 14: Dam Safety Performance Monitoring Program (DSPMP)

## USACE Engineering Regulations

### ER 1110-2-1156 — Safety of Dams: Policy and Procedures (2014)
Governs safety evaluation of USACE-owned and operated dams. Establishes:
- Dam Safety Action Classification (DSAC) ratings I through V
- Periodic Assessment requirements
- Issue Evaluation Study process
- Risk assessment methodology

### DSAC Ratings
- **DSAC I**: Urgent — dam safety issue confirmed, risk is extremely high
- **DSAC II**: Urgent and Compelling — high likelihood of failure under normal or loading conditions
- **DSAC III**: High Priority — moderate to high risk
- **DSAC IV**: Priority — risk exists but is not high
- **DSAC V**: Normal — acceptable level of risk

## ICOLD Publications

### Bulletin 158 — Dam Surveillance Guide (2018)
International best practices for dam monitoring and surveillance. Covers:
- Surveillance program design
- Instrumentation selection and installation
- Data management and interpretation
- Warning systems and emergency preparedness

### Bulletin 175 — Dam Safety Management (2020)
Framework for comprehensive dam safety management including:
- Organizational responsibilities
- Risk assessment integration
- Safety reviews and audits
- Continuous improvement processes
