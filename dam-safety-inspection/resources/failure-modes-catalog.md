# Dam Failure Modes Catalog

## Embankment Dam Failure Modes

### FM-E1: Backward Erosion Piping Through Foundation
- **Description**: Seepage exits at the downstream toe with sufficient gradient to initiate erosion of foundation soils, progressing upstream toward the reservoir
- **Initiating Conditions**: High reservoir level, permeable foundation layer, inadequate seepage cutoff
- **Visual Indicators**: Sand boils at downstream toe, turbid seepage water, localized wet areas, sinkholes
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Rising piezometric levels in foundation, increasing seepage flow with soil transport
- **Typical Progression**: Hours to days once piping channel is established

### FM-E2: Backward Erosion Piping Through Embankment
- **Description**: Seepage exits on the downstream face or at conduit penetrations, initiating erosion within the embankment fill
- **Initiating Conditions**: High reservoir level, cracks or preferential seepage paths, animal burrows, root channels
- **Visual Indicators**: Wet spots on downstream face, turbid seepage at toe, sinkholes on crest or upstream face
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Elevated pore pressures, increasing seepage flow
- **Typical Progression**: Hours to days

### FM-E3: Concentrated Leak Erosion Along Conduit
- **Description**: Seepage concentrated along the exterior of a conduit or other embedded structure erodes embankment material
- **Initiating Conditions**: Poor compaction around conduit, differential settlement creating gap, conduit joint separation
- **Visual Indicators**: Seepage carrying soil at conduit outlet, settlement above conduit alignment, wet areas along conduit trace
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Localized elevated pore pressures near conduit

### FM-E4: Internal Erosion by Suffusion
- **Description**: Fine particles are selectively removed from a broadly graded or gap-graded soil by seepage flow, leaving a coarser skeleton
- **Initiating Conditions**: Internally unstable soils (gap-graded or broadly graded), sufficient hydraulic gradient
- **Visual Indicators**: Turbid seepage, settlement over time as fines are removed
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Increasing permeability (can appear as decreasing piezometric levels), increased seepage flow

### FM-E5: Overtopping Erosion
- **Description**: Reservoir level exceeds dam crest elevation, flow erodes downstream face and unravels the embankment
- **Initiating Conditions**: Inadequate spillway capacity, spillway blockage, extreme flood event
- **Visual Indicators**: Water flowing over crest, erosion of downstream face
- **Progression**: Rapid once breach initiates — often minutes to hours

### FM-E6: Upstream Slope Instability (Rapid Drawdown)
- **Description**: Upstream slope fails when reservoir is drawn down faster than pore pressures in the embankment can dissipate
- **Initiating Conditions**: Rapid reservoir drawdown, low-permeability upstream shell, steep slopes
- **Visual Indicators**: Tension cracks on crest, bulging of upstream face, displacement of upstream toe

### FM-E7: Downstream Slope Instability (Steady-State Seepage)
- **Description**: Downstream slope fails due to elevated phreatic surface from sustained high reservoir levels
- **Initiating Conditions**: Prolonged high pool, inadequate drainage, high phreatic surface
- **Visual Indicators**: Bulging of downstream face, tension cracks on crest, wet or saturated downstream slope

## Concrete Dam Failure Modes

### FM-C1: Sliding on Foundation
- **Description**: Horizontal forces (hydrostatic + uplift) exceed shear resistance along the dam-foundation interface
- **Initiating Conditions**: High reservoir, excessive uplift pressure, weak foundation layer, reduced drain effectiveness
- **Visual Indicators**: Horizontal displacement of dam relative to foundation, opening of contraction joints, displaced survey monuments
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Increasing uplift pressures, horizontal movement detected by pendulum or survey

### FM-C2: Overturning
- **Description**: Overturning moment from hydrostatic and uplift forces exceeds resisting moment from dam weight
- **Initiating Conditions**: High reservoir, excessive uplift, reduced drain efficiency
- **Visual Indicators**: Downstream tilting, tension cracking at upstream heel
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Foundation uplift pressure increase, tilt measurements

### FM-C3: Alkali-Silica Reaction (ASR)
- **Description**: Chemical reaction between alkalis in cement and reactive silica in aggregates causes progressive expansion and cracking of concrete
- **Initiating Conditions**: Reactive aggregates, sufficient alkali content, moisture availability
- **Visual Indicators**: Map cracking (pattern cracking), gel exudation, misalignment of joints, displacement of structural features
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Progressive upstream-downstream or vertical growth detected by survey or extensometers

### FM-C4: Foundation Erosion / Dissolution
- **Description**: Foundation material is dissolved or eroded by seepage flow beneath the dam
- **Initiating Conditions**: Soluble foundation (limestone, gypsum), high seepage gradients
- **Visual Indicators**: Increasing leakage, sinkholes in reservoir area, turbid drain flow
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Increasing drain flow, changing uplift pressure distribution

### FM-C5: Arch Dam Abutment Instability
- **Description**: Thrust forces from the arch exceed the capacity of the abutment rock mass
- **Initiating Conditions**: Unfavorable geologic structure (joints, faults, weak layers), elevated water pressures in abutment
- **Visual Indicators**: Displacement of abutment rock, opening of joints, displacement of survey points
- **Instrumentation Indicators**: Abutment displacement detected by survey or extensometers, elevated abutment piezometric pressures

## Spillway and Outlet Failure Modes

### FM-S1: Spillway Erosion / Headcutting
- **Description**: Unlined or inadequately lined spillway erodes during flood passage, potentially undermining the dam
- **Initiating Conditions**: Flood exceeding design capacity, erodible foundation, inadequate energy dissipation
- **Visual Indicators**: Erosion of spillway channel, undermining of chute slab, headcut migration

### FM-S2: Outlet Works Gate Failure
- **Description**: Gate fails to operate when needed for reservoir regulation or emergency drawdown
- **Initiating Conditions**: Mechanical failure, sedimentation, corrosion, loss of power
- **Visual Indicators**: Gate inoperability during testing, excessive corrosion, sedimentation in approach channel
