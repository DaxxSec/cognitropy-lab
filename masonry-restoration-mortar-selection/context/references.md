# Masonry Restoration Mortar Selection — References

Compact lookup data for keying, naming, mix design and forecasting. Defer to the linked upstream sources for full specs.

## Binder classes (the phylogeny, soft → hard)

| Clade | Examples | Hydraulic Index | Set | Vapour-openness |
|------|----------|-----------------|-----|-----------------|
| Air (non-hydraulic) lime | CL 90 putty, hot-mixed fat lime, DL dolomitic | < 0.10 | Carbonation only | Highest |
| Pozzolan-gauged / feebly hydraulic | air lime + cocciopesto / trass / metakaolin | 0.10–0.16 | Carbonation + pozzolanic | High |
| Moderately hydraulic | NHL 2, NHL 3.5 | 0.16–0.31 | Hydration + carbonation | Medium |
| Eminently hydraulic | NHL 5 | 0.31–0.42 | Hydration-dominant | Lower |
| Natural / Roman cement | Prompt natural cement | > ~0.42 | Fast hydration | Low |
| Portland-cement mortar | OPC, CEM I gauges (ASTM Type M/S) | n/a (alite) | Rapid hydration | Lowest (the distant clade) |

## NHL grades (EN 459-1) — 28-day compressive strength bands

| Class | Strength (MPa) | Typical use |
|-------|----------------|-------------|
| NHL 2 | 2–7 | Sheltered, soft brick/stone, internal |
| NHL 3.5 | 3.5–10 | General external, moderate exposure |
| NHL 5 | 5–15 | Severe exposure, copings, below DPC — use sparingly on historic fabric |

Pick the **lowest** class compatible with substrate + exposure. CL = calcium lime (CL 90/80/70 by % CaO+MgO); DL = dolomitic.

## Mix-ratio cheat-sheet (by volume, binder:aggregate)

| Mortar | Typical ratio | Note |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Fat-lime putty:sand | 1:2.5 – 1:3 | Well-graded sharp sand; "fat" lime |
| Hot-mixed lime | ~1:3 quicklime:sand | Mixed hot; traditional, increasingly favoured |
| Pozzolan-gauged (cocciopesto) | 1 lime : 0.5–1 pozzolan : 6 sand | Faster set, mild hydraulicity |
| NHL:sand | 1:2.5 – 1:3 | Match grading; don't over-water |
| **Avoid for historic** | 1 cement : x sand | Too hard, too closed |

Rule of thumb: **voids ratio** — sand voids ≈ binder volume (so the binder fills voids without floating the aggregate).

## Analytical methods quick-ref

- **Acid digestion (dilute HCl)** → binder:aggregate + insoluble-residue grading. ⚠ calcareous aggregate dissolves → overstates binder.
- **Thin-section petrography (PLM)** → binder character, aggregate, grading, voids, point-counted ratio.
- **SEM-EDS / XRF** → oxide chemistry → Hydraulic Index.
- **XRD** → mineral phases (calcite, portlandite, ettringite, gypsum, C-S-H).
- **TGA** → bound water (~100–200 °C), portlandite (~400–550 °C), CaCO₃ (~600–800 °C).
- **MIP** → pore-size distribution (frost/salt durability).

## Decay-indicator failure thresholds (starter heuristics — tune per site)

| Indicator | Watch | Intervene |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Joint recession depth | > 6 mm behind face | > 1/3 joint depth or unit arris exposed |
| Capillary absorption (Karsten) | Rising trend | > 2× baseline / sound mortar |
| Soluble salt content | Detectable subflorescence | Spalling + salts > a few % |
| Biofilm coverage | > 30% | Tracking a defect + moisture confirmed |
| Scratch / drilling resistance | Softening trend | Friable, loses grains by hand |
| Monitoring epochs before trending | ≥ 3 | ≥ 4 for a forecast with a band |
