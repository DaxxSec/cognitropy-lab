# digitization-charter

## Purpose

Draft a one-page digitization-project charter from goals and constraints — the document that fixes scope, standard, capacity assumptions, and the optimization policy *before* work starts, so the queue and schedule have an agreed basis. Use at project kickoff or grant-application stage.

## Prompt Template

```
You are scoping a cultural-heritage digitization project and need a defensible charter.

Project context:

- **Sponsor / funder & goal:** [WHO WANTS THIS AND WHY — ACCESS, PRESERVATION, EXHIBITION, GRANT DELIVERABLE]
- **Collections in scope:** [WHAT IS / ISN'T INCLUDED]
- **Target imaging standard:** [FADGI ?★ / METAMORFOZE ? / ISO 19264 LEVEL ?]
- **Capacity:** [STATIONS, OPERATOR FTE, HOURS/WEEK, BUDGET, DEADLINE]
- **Prioritization policy:** [PRESERVATION-FIRST / ACCESS-FIRST / DEMAND-DRIVEN — OR ASK ME]
- **Constraints:** [NO-TRANSIT MATERIAL, RIGHTS RESTRICTIONS, ENVIRONMENTAL LIMITS]

Please produce a charter with:
1. **Objectives & success criteria** — measurable (e.g. "N images at FADGI 4★, all as OAIS AIPs with fixity").
2. **Scope & explicit exclusions.**
3. **Standard & deliverable spec** — master format, metadata, packaging, access deliverables.
4. **Optimization policy** — the value weights and any hard gates (no-transit, urgency overrides), stated as governance choices.
5. **Capacity & high-level timeline** — with the utilization discount applied, and the binding-constraint assumption.
6. **Risks & mitigations** — fragile material, throughput uncertainty, storage, rights.
```

## Expected Output

- A concise charter (≈ 1 page) suitable for a sponsor or funder.
- An explicit, defensible **optimization policy** (weights + hard gates) that downstream commands inherit.
- A realistic capacity/timeline statement with assumptions and risks named, not hidden.
