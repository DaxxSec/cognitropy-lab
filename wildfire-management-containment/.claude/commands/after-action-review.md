# /after-action-review — Operational-Period AAR

Run a structured NWCG-style After Action Review for an operational period or shift — plan vs. actual, what to sustain, what to improve — so the next period and the next fire are safer and tighter.

## Inputs

- The operational period's plan/objectives (IAP, containment plan, triage decisions)
- What actually happened: line built, structures saved/lost, spread vs. projection, any near-misses or safety events
- Resource performance and logistics notes
- Participants (the crew/division involved)

## Steps

1. **What was planned?** Restate the period's objectives and key decisions (strategy, triage calls, evacuation triggers).
2. **What actually happened?** Lay out the facts without blame — perimeter outcome, structures, spread vs. projection, timing.
3. **Why the difference?** Analyze where plan and reality diverged (weather not as forecast, production rate off, spotting underestimated, comms gap).
4. **What do we sustain?** Identify what worked and should be repeated.
5. **What do we improve?** Identify concrete, assignable fixes — not "be more careful" but "post the lookout on the knob, not the road."
6. Capture any **safety events / near-misses** and route them per the SAFENET/learning process.
7. Feed improvements forward into the next period's plan.

## Output

An AAR to `outputs/aar-<date>-<period>.md` with the four AAR questions (planned / actual / why / sustain-improve), a safety-event note, and a short list of assignable improvements for the next period.

## Notes

- AAR is blameless by design — the goal is learning, not fault. Facts first, judgment second.
- "Sustain" matters as much as "improve" — name what worked so it isn't lost on the next rotation.
- Near-misses are the cheapest lessons available; capture them every time, even on a clean shift.
