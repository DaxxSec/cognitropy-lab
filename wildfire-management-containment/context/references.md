# Wildfire Management & Containment — Reference Tables

Compact lookup data the agent reaches for during a task. Numbers here are decision thresholds and standard interpretations; defer to BehavePlus, the IRPG, and local fire-danger operating plans for authoritative values.

## Flame Length → Fireline Intensity → Suppression Interpretation ("Hauling Chart")

Andrews & Rothermel interpretation. Fireline intensity *I* (Btu/ft/s) relates to flame length *L* (ft) by Byram's equation `L = 0.45 · I^0.46` (≈ `I = (L/0.45)^(1/0.46)`).

| Flame length (ft) | Fireline intensity (Btu/ft/s) | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| < 4 | < ~100 | Direct attack at the head/flanks by persons with handtools; handline holds. |
| 4 – 8 | ~100 – 500 | Too intense for direct attack by persons with handtools. Dozers, engines, retardant, or indirect. |
| 8 – 11 | ~500 – 1000 | Serious control problems: torching, spotting, crowning. Direct control efforts at the head likely ineffective. |
| > 11 | > ~1000 | Crowning, major spotting, extreme behavior. Control efforts at the head ineffective — go indirect / protect values / wait for change. |

## Structure Triage Categories (NWCG / IRPG)

| Category | Defensible? | Crew action | Gate condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Defensible — Stand Alone | Yes | Patrol / check; do not commit | Good survivable space + hardened; survives without forces |
| Defensible — Prep & Hold | Yes | Improve & defend through front | Safety zone + escape route present; survivable space |
| Non-Defensible — Prep & Leave | No | Quick prep if safe, then leave before front | No safety zone OR poor space/construction; some time |
| Non-Defensible — Rescue Drive-By | No | Life rescue only; no property defense | No time / immediate hazard; unsafe to commit |

## Dead Fuel Moisture — Behavior Guidance (1-hr fuels)

| 1-hr fuel moisture | Expected behavior |
|---|---|
| > 17% | Marginal ignition/spread; fire generally manageable |
| 11–17% | Moderate spread; standard tactics |
| 8–11% | Active spread; spotting begins |
| 5–8% | Rapid spread, frequent spotting; aggressive behavior |
| < 5% | Extreme; expect crowning, long-range spotting, plume dominance |

*(Live fuel moisture matters seasonally; chaparral LFM < ~60–80% is a critical-burning threshold.)*

## Approximate Line Production Rates (planning only — terrain/fuel dependent)

| Resource | Handline / line rate (chains/hr) | Notes (1 chain = 66 ft) |
|---|---|---|
| Type 1 hotshot crew (20) | ~20–40+ (light fuels) | Highest sustained handline production |
| Type 2 crew (20) | ~10–20 | General-purpose |
| Single engine module | ~2–5 handline | Better at structure protection / mobile attack |
| Dozer (Type 2) | ~30–100+ | Terrain-limited; fast in accessible ground |
| Dozer (Type 1, heavy) | ~60–150+ | Steep/rocky reduces sharply |

Production drops with slope, heavy fuels, rock, and night ops. Always treat as order-of-magnitude.

## Containment Lifecycle Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| % contained | Fraction of perimeter with completed, holding control line |
| Controlled | Entire perimeter secured; no further spread expected |
| Mop-up | Extinguishing/cooling heat within gridded distance of line |
| Cold-trailing | Hand-checking the line for residual heat |
| Out | No detectable heat; incident closed |
| Slop-over / breakover | Fire crossing the line at a point |
| Spot fire | New ignition ahead of the fire from embers |

## Red Flag Warning — Typical Criteria (NWS; varies by region)

- Sustained wind ≥ ~15–25 mph and/or frequent gusts ≥ ~25–35 mph
- Relative humidity ≤ ~15% (region-specific)
- Dry/cured fuels (often gated by ERC or fuel-moisture percentiles)
- Dry lightning / abrupt wind shift (frontal passage)
- Issued when a critical combination is expected within ~24 hr.

## Quick Safety Cheat-Sheet

- **LCES before commitment, every time.** Lookout posted, Comms tested, Escape routes timed (and re-timed as fire behavior changes), Safety zone sized for flame length.
- **Anchor first.** Never build line without an anchor point.
- **One foot in the black.** Work from / retreat to the burned area.
- **Brief the wind shift.** A forecast direction change is the most important line in any briefing.
- **Watch Out profile:** small fire, light/flashy fuels, slope <30%, unfamiliar terrain, downhill line — the deceptive tragedy-fire setup.
