## Why This Workspace

Protective movement planning is high-consequence and rarely audited. Detail leaders pick routes based on experience and a feel for the terrain, but the *reasoning* almost never survives the movement: there's no record of what threats were considered, why one route beat another, or which mitigations were credited. When a movement goes well it teaches nothing; when one goes badly the lessons are often lost in incident-report prose.

A formal 5×5 risk scoring matrix — long standard in safety-critical industries (aviation, nuclear, oil & gas, ISO 31000) — is the right tool to drag this work into a defensible, teachable, comparable form. Every segment of every candidate route is plotted on the same axes, every mitigation produces a documented residual score, and route selection becomes a multi-criteria comparison anyone on the detail can read and challenge.
