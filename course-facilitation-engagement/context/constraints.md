# Constraints & Boundaries

> Populated by `/onboard` — edit as needed.

## Privacy & Consent
- Engagement signals (login, completion, forum activity, pulse responses) are personal data; collect under explicit enrollment consent
- Default to learner-aggregate reporting; learner-individual reporting only with documented consent
- Manager visibility into individual signals is opt-in by the learner unless the program is mandatory and consent is part of enrollment
- Honor regional privacy law in scope (GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, PIPEDA, APPI, LGPD)
- Retention policy must match the org policy; default to delete behavioral traces 12 months after cohort close

## Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 AA for any learner-facing copy and any sponsor-facing artifact
- Captions and transcripts for any embedded video
- Color is never the only signal in a chart (also use shape, label, or pattern)
- Plain-language target — Grade 8 reading level on learner copy unless the audience is technical

## Brand Voice & Approved Language
- Reference the org's brand voice doc; if absent, use a "warm, direct, peer-to-peer" tone
- Maintain an approved-terms / banned-terms list under `resources/` if regulated
- Avoid coercive framing in any manager-targeted message

## Compliance
- Identify the named compliance reviewer(s) and the review-window SLA
- Flag templates that touch regulated topics (financial advice, health, safety) for mandatory review
- Calendar must include "freeze windows" before regulator filings or earnings periods if applicable

## Channel Etiquette
- No mass DM blast — channel announcements first, DM only for individualized nudges
- Time-zone aware sends; never schedule a triggered SMS outside 08:00–20:00 local
- Throttle nudges — never more than two messages to the same learner in 48 hours unless the learner opts in to higher cadence

## Out of Scope
- Performance management actions (this workspace supports learning engagement, not HR discipline)
- Bypassing the LMS or org messaging policy (templates are drafted here, sent via approved systems)
- Sentiment surveillance — pulse responses are pseudonymized; only aggregate is used for cohort decisions
