# Engagement Frameworks — Quick Reference

The frameworks the agent applies when reading a signal, choosing a template, or deciding whether a sponsor readout is honest.

## 1. Community of Inquiry (Garrison, Anderson & Archer, 2000)

Three presences must co-exist for a learner cohort to thrive:

| Presence | What it is | What it sounds like in copy |
|----------|------------|----------------------------|
| Cognitive | Sense-making, knowledge construction | "Here's the question we'll try to answer", "Try this with your real situation" |
| Social | Identification, belonging, expression of personality | Named recognition, peer prompts, "we" language, facilitator self-disclosure |
| Teaching | Design, facilitation, direction | Objectives, scaffolding, pace markers, summaries |

**Operational rule:** if a week's outbound copy is ≥75% one presence, the cadence is unbalanced. Most failure modes are runaway teaching presence — long emails packed with logistics and direction with no social or cognitive prompts.

References: [coi.athabascau.ca](https://coi.athabascau.ca/) — instruments and validated rubrics for each presence.

## 2. Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)

Sustained motivation depends on three universal psychological needs:

| Driver | What it is | How copy supports it |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Autonomy | Acting in line with one's values; choice | "Choose how you apply", "What works for you?", graceful exits |
| Competence | Sense of effectiveness, growth | Specific positive feedback, scaffolded next steps |
| Relatedness | Connection with others | Peer prompts, named cohort references, facilitator-as-human cues |

**Operational rule:** if a draft uses pressure verbs ("must", "required") or strips choice, it erodes autonomy and backfires. Compliance language is permissible only when an actual compliance constraint exists — and even then, frame the constraint, not the coercion.

References: [selfdeterminationtheory.org](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/) — meta-analyses across education, healthcare, and workplace contexts.

## 3. Kirkpatrick's Four Levels (and the New World Model)

Evaluation cascade:

| Level | What it measures | Common trap |
|------:|------------------|-------------|
| 1 | Reaction (engagement, satisfaction, NPS, pulse) | Reporting only Level 1 to executive sponsors |
| 2 | Learning (knowledge / skill / attitude delta) | Skipping pre-assessment, forcing post-only design |
| 3 | Behavior (on-the-job application at D+30 to D+90) | "We'll measure later" — never collected |
| 4 | Results (business metric, lead/lag) | Claiming attribution without controls |

**Operational rule:** Every cohort defines its Level 3 instrument before the cohort starts (manager check-ins, application plans, peer-reviewed artifacts). Level 4 is supportable only with leading indicators and named confidence.

The New World Model adds the leading indicators of Required Drivers (reinforcement, accountability, monitoring, rewards) — those are exactly what stakeholder communication templates instantiate.

References: [kirkpatrickpartners.com](https://www.kirkpatrickpartners.com/the-kirkpatrick-model/).

## 4. Five Moments of Need (Mosher & Gottfredson)

Performance-support framing for triggered messaging:

| Moment | What is happening | Right template intent |
|--------|------------------|-----------------------|
| New | First encounter | invite, scaffolded prework |
| More | Building depth | nudge, deeper artifact |
| Apply | In the workflow | recap, application prompt |
| Solve | Something broke | escalate, office hours |
| Change | Context shifts | reset (silent cohort revival) |

**Operational rule:** triggered messages target the moment, not the calendar. A "More" template sent during a "Solve" moment makes the learner feel unheard.

References: [5momentsofneed.com](https://www.5momentsofneed.com/).

## 5. Power–Interest Matrix (Mendelow, 1991)

Four quadrants for cadence depth:

| | Low interest | High interest |
|---|---|---|
| **High power** | Keep satisfied — milestone-driven, never deluge | Manage closely — full cadence, named approver |
| **Low power** | Monitor — single inform per phase boundary | Keep informed — full content, no decision asks |

Use to set baseline cadence; use the salience model below for in-period changes.

## 6. Stakeholder Salience (Mitchell, Agle & Wood, 1997)

Three attributes — Power, Legitimacy, Urgency — give seven combinations:

- **Definitive** (P + L + U) — full cadence depth, named approver, two-day SLA
- **Dominant** (P + L) — full cadence
- **Dependent** (L + U) — supportive cadence; surface to a Definitive
- **Discretionary** (L only) — milestone touchpoints
- **Dangerous** (P + U) — flag and resolve; rare but real
- **Demanding** (U only) — single response then archive
- **Dormant** (P only) — monitor for activation

Salience can shift mid-cohort. Re-rank on every phase boundary.

Reference: Mitchell, R. K., Agle, B. R., & Wood, D. J. (1997). *Toward a theory of stakeholder identification and salience.* Academy of Management Review, 22(4), 853-886. [JSTOR 259247](https://www.jstor.org/stable/259247)

## 7. Universal Design for Learning (UDL)

Three principles, each with multiple means:

| Principle | Multiple means of… | Practical channel choice |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| Representation | Perception, language, comprehension | Text + Loom + transcript; alt text on visuals |
| Action & Expression | Physical action, expression, executive function | Choice between writing, recording, building |
| Engagement | Recruiting interest, sustaining effort, self-regulation | Choice in prompts, optional peer-pair, opt-out lanes |

**Operational rule:** every learner-facing module recap pairs text with at least one alternate representation. UDL is not optional copy-improvement; it is the floor.

Reference: [CAST UDL Guidelines](https://udlguidelines.cast.org/).

## 8. Knowles' Andragogy

Six adult-learning principles (1980):

1. Adults need to know *why* they are learning something
2. Adults learn experientially
3. Adults approach learning as problem-solving
4. Adults learn best when the topic is of immediate value
5. Adults need to be involved in planning their learning
6. Adults are internally motivated more than externally

**Operational rule:** every learner template leads with the *why*, not the logistics. Logistics belong below the relevance.

## 9. Plain-Language Guidelines (plainlanguage.gov)

Checklist for any send:

- Active voice as default
- Sentences ≤25 words on average; many shorter
- Common words; if a term-of-art appears, gloss it once
- "You" and "we" — never "the learner", "the participant"
- Calls to action have a verb, an object, and a deadline
- Lists for parallel items, paragraphs for linked thought
- Headings every 100–150 words on long copy
- Reading-level target Grade 8 for learner-facing

Reference: [Federal Plain Language Guidelines](https://www.plainlanguage.gov/guidelines/).

## 10. WCAG 2.1 AA — Practical Floor for Learner Copy

- Color contrast ≥4.5:1 for normal text, ≥3:1 for large text
- Color is never the only signal in a chart (also use shape, label, pattern)
- Images carry meaningful alt text; decorative images carry empty alt
- Headings are real headings (`<h1>`, `<h2>`) — no styled bold pretending to be a heading
- Focus order is logical for keyboard users
- Captions and transcripts on any video
- Form fields have labels; error messages identify the field

Reference: [W3C WCAG 2.1](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG21/).

## 11. Csikszentmihalyi's Flow Conditions

For module pacing — flow needs:
- Clear goals (one focus question per module)
- Immediate feedback (peer or facilitator response within the module)
- Skill–challenge balance (don't make it trivial; don't make it impossible)

**Operational rule:** if learners report "boredom" use the skill–challenge mismatch lens (challenge too low) before assuming low motivation.

## 12. Honest Use Notes

- Frameworks are tools, not commandments. If the cohort culture conflicts with a framework's prescription (e.g., facilitator self-disclosure is uncomfortable in some senior-leader cohorts), adjust the prescription, not the framework.
- One framework per copy decision is enough. Tagging a single template with all four frameworks is theater, not rigor.
- Sponsors do not want frameworks. They want decisions and evidence. Use frameworks privately to design the cadence; report results plainly.

## Plain-Language Checklist (paste into a render)

```
[ ] Active voice in 80%+ of sentences
[ ] Average sentence length ≤25 words
[ ] No jargon without a one-line gloss
[ ] "You" and "we" — no third person
[ ] One ask, one verb, one deadline
[ ] Reading-level Grade 8 (learner) or org-target (other audiences)
[ ] One alternate representation paired with the text artifact
[ ] Graceful exit clause on any nudge
[ ] No `{{placeholders}}` remaining
```
