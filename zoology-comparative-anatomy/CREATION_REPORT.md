## Why this workspace

Zoology comparative anatomy is one of biology's oldest, most data-rich, and most underspecified-by-modern-tools disciplines. Despite a 200-year tradition of structured description, many comparative-anatomy projects still produce idiosyncratic notes that other workers can't reuse. The technique pairing for this build — **standardized inspection checklists** — is the explicit antidote: every inspection runs the same checklist, every comparison cites the same vocabulary, every homology claim is grounded in topology + ontogeny + phylogeny. The result is records that survive past the original investigator and feed directly into trait matrices, manuscripts, and curatorial catalogs.
