# Invasive Species Reporting Platforms Guide

> How to submit your field observations to the right platforms to contribute meaningful data.

---

## iNaturalist
**URL:** https://www.inaturalist.org | **App:** iNaturalist (iOS/Android)
**Best for:** Any organism — community ID assistance, broad biodiversity data
**Key steps:**
1. Tap "+" → Add observation
2. Add photos (multiple angles — the community uses them for ID)
3. Set date and location (GPS auto-fill or map pin)
4. Add your best species guess (even "Plants" is fine — the community will refine)
5. Add notes: habitat, behavior, any relevant context
6. Mark "No" for captive/cultivated

**Pro tips:**
- Join your state's iNaturalist invasive species project for direct routing
- "Needs ID" observations get reviewed by experts — valuable even without confident ID
- Observation Quality Grade "Research Grade" = identified by 2+ people in agreement → feeds GBIF database

---

## EDDMapS — Early Detection & Distribution Mapping System
**URL:** https://www.eddmaps.org | **App:** EDDMapS (iOS/Android)
**Best for:** Invasive plants, animals, pathogens — most important for land management decisions
**Required fields:**
- Species (scientific name preferred — use dropdown)
- Observation date
- GPS coordinates (decimal degrees)
- State and county
- Abundance: isolated / few / many / abundant
- Any photos

**Tips:**
- Account required for submission (free)
- Check the map before submitting — if you're far outside known established range, expect follow-up from state coordinator
- Download the app for offline data entry, sync when connected

---

## iMapInvasives
**URL:** https://imapinvasives.natureserve.org
**Best for:** State-level programs — many state invasive species councils use this as their primary database
**Note:** Platform varies by state — check if your state participates before creating account

---

## USDA APHIS ReportAPest
**URL:** https://www.aphis.usda.gov/plant_health/plant_pest_info/pest_detection/reportpest.shtml
**Best for:** Federally regulated pests — spotted lanternfly, emerald ash borer, sudden oak death, etc.
**When to use:** First-time detection of a Tier 1 species in a new county or state
**Also contact:** Your state department of agriculture directly for fastest response

---

## eBird (Cornell Lab of Ornithology)
**URL:** https://ebird.org | **App:** eBird
**Best for:** Bird observations — important for tracking wildlife indicator species
**Note:** Bird absence/presence is valuable for ecosystem health monitoring

## Photo Standards for Maximum Usefulness

For any observation, these photo angles get the best ID results:

### Plants
1. **Whole plant habit** — step back, show the entire plant in context
2. **Leaf top** — close up of upper leaf surface, showing shape and veins
3. **Leaf bottom** — hairs, color, venation pattern
4. **Stem close-up** — showing texture, hair, markings, cross-section if broken
5. **Flower/fruit detail** (if present)
6. **Root** (if accessible without destructive sampling)
7. **Scale reference** — hand, pen, ruler for size

### Insects
1. **Dorsal view** — top of insect
2. **Lateral view** — side profile
3. **Habitat context** — where you found it, what it was on

### Mushrooms
1. **Cap top**
2. **Cap underside** — gills, pores, or teeth
3. **Stem with base** — show base and any ring/volva
4. **Cross-section cut** — color change on flesh exposure?
5. **Habitat** — what substrate, associated trees

## Offline Workflow for No-Service Areas

When you're out of cell range:

1. **Mark GPS waypoint** with descriptive name (e.g., "INVASIVE_01_KNOTWEED")
2. **Take photos** with location services on (saves GPS to EXIF data)
3. **Record observation notes** in notebook or voice memo:
   - Species or description
   - Estimated abundance
   - Habitat type
   - Notable features
4. **On return to connectivity:**
   - Upload photos to iNaturalist (GPS auto-populates from EXIF)
   - Submit EDDMapS report
   - Update work-log and context/project.md
