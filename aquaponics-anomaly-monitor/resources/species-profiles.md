# Fish Species Profiles

Reference data for commonly raised aquaponics species.

---

## Tilapia (Oreochromis niloticus and related)

**Best for:** Warm climates, beginners, high-density systems
**Temperature:** 25–30°C optimal, tolerates 15–35°C, lethal <12°C
**pH:** 6.5–8.5 (very tolerant), optimal 7.0–8.0
**NH3 tolerance:** 0.5 ppm max, prefer <0.1 ppm
**NO2- tolerance:** 0.5 ppm max
**DO minimum:** 4 mg/L (air-breathers — can tolerate low DO better than most)
**Stocking density:** Up to 50 kg/m³ with good aeration, 20–30 kg/m³ recommended
**Feed rate:** 1–3% body weight/day depending on temperature and size
**Time to harvest:** 6–9 months to 500–600g

**Notes:** Extremely hardy. Will tell you when stressed by grouping near surface or inlet. Breed prolifically — control with single-sex stocking.

---

## Rainbow Trout (Oncorhynchus mykiss)

**Best for:** Cold climates, higher value product, experienced operators
**Temperature:** 12–18°C optimal, lethal >24°C sustained
**pH:** 6.5–8.0, optimal 7.0–7.5
**NH3 tolerance:** 0.3 ppm max — more sensitive than tilapia
**NO2- tolerance:** 0.2 ppm max — sensitive to nitrogen compounds
**DO minimum:** 7 mg/L (critical — highly oxygen-demanding)
**Stocking density:** 20–40 kg/m³ with exceptional aeration
**Feed rate:** 1–2% body weight/day (varies with water temperature)
**Time to harvest:** 12–18 months to 400–500g

**Notes:** Temperature-critical. A warm summer can kill an entire batch. Require very clean water. Monitor DO obsessively.

---

## Yellow Perch (Perca flavescens)

**Best for:** North American temperate climates, premium market
**Temperature:** 18–24°C optimal, tolerates 4–28°C
**pH:** 6.5–8.5, optimal 7.0–8.0
**NH3 tolerance:** 0.5 ppm max
**NO2- tolerance:** 0.3 ppm
**DO minimum:** 5 mg/L
**Stocking density:** 20–40 kg/m³
**Feed rate:** 1.5–2.5% body weight/day
**Time to harvest:** 18–24 months (slow growing)

**Notes:** High market value but slow growth. Semi-annual spawning complicates production scheduling.

---

## Channel Catfish (Ictalurus punctatus)

**Best for:** Warm climates, high tolerance operators
**Temperature:** 24–30°C optimal, tolerates 4–35°C
**pH:** 6.5–8.5
**NH3 tolerance:** 0.5 ppm max
**NO2- tolerance:** 0.5 ppm
**DO minimum:** 4 mg/L (air-breathing capability via labyrinth organ)
**Stocking density:** 30–50 kg/m³
**Feed rate:** 2–3% body weight/day
**Time to harvest:** 12–18 months

**Notes:** Highly resilient. Good for warm, dense systems. Whiskers (barbels) sense chemical gradients — behavioral changes are early warning signs.

---

## Common Carp (Cyprinus carpio)

**Best for:** Traditional systems, polyculture, Asian markets
**Temperature:** 20–28°C optimal, tolerates 4–35°C
**pH:** 6.5–8.0
**NH3 tolerance:** 0.4 ppm
**NO2- tolerance:** 0.4 ppm
**DO minimum:** 5 mg/L
**Stocking density:** 20–30 kg/m³
**Feed rate:** 2–3% body weight/day
**Time to harvest:** 12–24 months

**Notes:** Very hardy but can disturb media beds by rooting. Good choice for integrated systems where they help turn over growing media.

## Plant-Side Reference

### Common Aquaponics Crops and Nutrient Preferences

| Crop | pH Preference | Key Nutrients | Grow Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lettuce | 6.0–7.0 | N, K, Ca | 30–45 days | Ideal starter crop |
| Basil | 6.0–7.0 | N, K, Mg | 45–60 days | High value, fast growth |
| Tomatoes | 5.5–6.5 | P, K, Ca, Mg | 60–90 days to fruit | Needs additional Fe |
| Cucumbers | 5.5–6.5 | K, Ca, Mg | 50–70 days | High nutrient demand |
| Kale/Greens | 6.0–7.5 | N, K | 30–50 days | Very tolerant |
| Herbs | 5.5–7.0 | N, K | 30–60 days | Many varieties work well |
| Strawberries | 5.5–6.5 | K, Ca, Mg | 90–120 days to fruit | Needs lower pH |
