# Troubleshooting Matrix

Quick-reference symptom → cause → action guide.

---

## Fish Symptoms

| Symptom | Most Likely Causes | Check These Parameters | Immediate Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fish at surface gasping | Low DO, high ammonia, gill damage | DO, NH3, NO2, pH | Emergency aeration first |
| Fish hiding / inactive | Ammonia, nitrite, pH stress, disease | NH3, NO2, pH | Full parameter check |
| Fish off-feed (>1 day) | NH3 >0.25 ppm, wrong temp, early disease | NH3, Temp, DO | Parameter check, watch closely |
| Erratic swimming / spinning | Bacterial infection, NH3 toxicity, parasite | NH3, NO2 | Isolate sick fish, treat |
| Red/inflamed gills | NH3 toxicity, low DO, bacterial/parasitic | NH3, DO, look for parasites | Water change, aeration |
| Swollen abdomen (dropsy) | Bacterial infection (internal) | Overall chemistry | Isolate; may need antibiotic |
| White spots (ich) | Ichthyophthirius parasite | Temp (stress trigger) | Salt treatment, temp raise |
| Fin rot | Bacterial infection, poor water quality | NH3, NO2, overall chemistry | Improve water quality; Melafix |

## Plant Symptoms

| Symptom | Most Likely Cause | Check/Test | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overall yellowing (chlorosis) | Iron deficiency (most common) | Fe test or add Fe | Add chelated iron (Fe-EDTA) |
| Old leaves yellow, new leaves green | Nitrogen or Magnesium deficiency | NO3, NH3 | Check fish loading; add Mg |
| New leaves yellow, old leaves green | Iron or Calcium deficiency | Fe, Ca | Add chelated iron or Cal-Mag |
| Purple/red leaves | Phosphorus deficiency or cold temps | P, temperature | Check fish loading |
| Slow growth, small leaves | Low nitrogen (low fish loading) | NO3 < 10 ppm | Increase feeding or fish density |
| Wilting despite adequate water | Root rot (Pythium) | Root inspection | Improve DO at roots; reduce pH |
| Brown leaf edges | High salt/EC, or Potassium deficiency | EC, K | Partial water change if EC high |

## System-Level Problems

| Problem | Causes | Parameters | Solution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ammonia won't come down | New tank cycling, biofilter crash, overcrowding, overfeeding | NH3, NO2, pH | Reduce feeding, partial water changes, check biofilter |
| NO2 spike with low NH3 | Stage 2 failure (Nitrobacter stressed) | NO2, pH, DO in filter | Check pH, DO; reduce load |
| pH keeps crashing | Low alkalinity (KH), high bioload | KH, NH3 load | Add potassium bicarbonate; buffer regularly |
| pH won't stabilize | Mineral imbalance, low KH | KH, EC | Add alkalinity buffer |
| DO always borderline | Warm water, insufficient aeration, high BOD | DO, Temp | Add aeration; reduce organic load |
| EC creeping up | Mineral accumulation, evaporation | EC, regular additions | Partial water change |
| EC falling | Dilution from rain/top-off, nutrient uptake | EC | Add mineral supplement |
| Algae bloom | Excess light + nutrients | NO3, P, light exposure | Cover tank, reduce NO3 |
| Snails (explosion) | Overfeeding, decaying matter | NH3 (indicator of excess organic) | Reduce feeding, manual removal |
| Water cloudiness | Bacterial bloom (new system), dead fish, pH swing | NH3, NO2, visual inspection | Find root cause; aeration helps |

## Emergency Quick-Reference

### Fish Kill Risk — Immediate Actions

1. **Check DO first** — add emergency aeration (air pump, cascade, bucket pour)
2. **Check NH3** — if >0.5 ppm, do immediate 20–30% water change
3. **Remove dead fish immediately** — they continue to raise ammonia
4. **Stop feeding** — do not add more ammonia load to a stressed system
5. **Do NOT add salt if using medication** — interactions possible
6. **Monitor every 2–4 hours** until stable

### pH Crash Emergency (pH < 6.5)

1. Do NOT add pH corrector rapidly — a pH swing can be as damaging as the crash
2. Target: raise by maximum 0.5 units/12 hours
3. Add sodium bicarbonate or potassium bicarbonate at 1g/10L increments
4. Monitor every 30 minutes after addition
5. Identify root cause (CO2, acid dose, low alkalinity) before committing to full correction

### Biofilter Crash Protocol

1. Immediately reduce feeding to 25% of normal
2. Daily 20–25% water changes until NH3 < 0.2 ppm
3. Do NOT clean biofilter
4. Restore optimal conditions: pH 7.0–7.4, DO >6, Temp in optimal range
5. Monitor every 12 hours
6. Optional: Add nitrifying bacteria supplement
7. Expect 2–3 weeks recovery; DO NOT increase feeding until NH3 + NO2 both stable <0.1 ppm for 72 hours
