# /surveillance-detection-plan

Design a surveillance-detection route (SDR) and a pre-attack-indicator watch list to detect hostile surveillance of the principal; generate EPA-5 evidence.

## Inputs

- Principal's routine movements and pattern-of-life
- Suspected surveillance reports or anomalies (if any)
- Map of the operating area with natural channel points
- Apprentice author + current EPA-5 entrustment level

## Steps

1. Design an **SDR** that channels any follower through points where they must commit (turns, stops, reversals) and reveal themselves through **time, environment, distance, demeanour (TEDD)**.
2. Build the **pre-attack-indicator (PIN) watch list**: casing, photography of security, probing questions, fixed observation posts, repeated TEDD presence.
3. Define **detection thresholds** and what a confirmed "hit" triggers (counter-surveillance activation, route change, LE referral).
4. Decide passive **SD** vs active **counter-surveillance** posture given the threat tier.
5. Record the **EPA-5** entrustment observation.

## Output

`outputs/sdr-<area>-<date>.md` — SDR route with commit points, PIN watch list, detection thresholds and triggers, SD/CS posture recommendation, and EPA-5 evidence footer.

## Notes

- SD is observational, not interdiction; the SDR's job is to *reveal* surveillance, not to confront it.
- A single TEDD coincidence is noise; the signal is repetition across independent legs.
