# /eap-builder

Build and table-top a scenario emergency action plan (attack-on-principal, medical, fire, vehicle), and use the rehearsal as a workplace-based assessment of EPA-7.

## Inputs

- Scenario(s) to plan for and the site/movement context (from advance or route work)
- Detail roster, comms plan, nearest Level I/II trauma center
- Apprentice author (plan) and junior agents (drill participants)

## Steps

1. For each scenario, write **immediate actions** in **get-off-the-X** order: sound off → cover → evacuate.
2. Assign **roles, triggers, rally points, comms primary/alternate**, and the **medical** route to the nearest trauma center.
3. Define decision authority and the hand-off to law enforcement / EMS.
4. **Table-top** the plan; observe junior agents' immediate-action competency under the scenario.
5. Capture an **AAR** (use `prompts/after-action-review.md`) blending operational fixes and individual competency observations.
6. Record the **EPA-7** observation for the plan author and immediate-action observations for the drill participants.

## Output

`outputs/eap-<scenario>-<site>-<date>.md` — scenario EAPs with immediate actions, role/trigger/rally/comms matrix, medical plan, AAR notes, and EPA-7 + immediate-action evidence footers.

## Notes

- A plan that has never been rehearsed is a hypothesis. The drill is where both the plan and the people are actually assessed.
