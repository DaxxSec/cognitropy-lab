# Regulatory & Legal Reference

## Key US Laws and Standards

### Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA)
- **Scope**: US issuers, domestic concerns, and any person in US territory
- **Anti-bribery**: Prohibits payments to foreign government officials for business advantage
- **Books & Records**: Public companies must maintain accurate books and adequate internal controls
- **Enforcement**: DOJ (criminal) and SEC (civil)
- **Penalties**: Up to $2M per violation (companies), $100K + 5 years (individuals)
- **Key cases**: Siemens ($800M, 2008), Goldman Sachs/1MDB ($2.9B, 2020)

### Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)
- **Section 302**: CEO/CFO must certify accuracy of financial statements quarterly
- **Section 404**: Management assessment and auditor attestation of internal controls over financial reporting
- **Section 806**: Whistleblower protections for public company employees
- **Section 1107**: Retaliation against whistleblowers is a federal crime
- **Applies to**: US public companies and their subsidiaries

### Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) / Anti-Money Laundering
- **Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)**: Required for cash transactions >$10,000
- **Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)**: Required when institution suspects money laundering or fraud; 30-day filing window
- **Structuring**: Illegal to break up transactions to avoid CTR threshold (31 CFR 1010.314)
- **Know Your Customer (KYC)**: Financial institutions must verify customer identity and beneficial ownership
- **Customer Due Diligence (CDD)**: Ongoing monitoring of customer relationships
- **Applies to**: Banks, credit unions, broker-dealers, MSBs, casinos

### Corporate Transparency Act (CTA) / FinCEN BOI
- **Effective**: January 1, 2024
- **Requirement**: Most US companies must report beneficial ownership to FinCEN
- **Beneficial owner**: Any individual with >25% ownership or substantial control
- **Penalties**: $500/day civil penalty; up to 2 years criminal
- **Exemptions**: Public companies, large operating companies, heavily regulated entities

### RICO (Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act)
- Allows civil and criminal prosecution of organized fraud patterns
- Requires predicate acts (mail/wire fraud, money laundering) forming a "pattern of racketeering"
- Provides treble damages in civil cases
- Often used in complex fraud schemes involving multiple participants

## Key Regulatory Bodies

| Agency | Jurisdiction | Fraud/Financial Crime Focus |
|---|---|---|
| DOJ / FBI | Federal criminal | Wire fraud, bank fraud, securities fraud, FCPA, RICO |
| SEC Enforcement | Securities laws | Financial statement fraud, insider trading, FCPA |
| IRS Criminal Investigation (IRS-CI) | Tax crimes | Tax fraud, money laundering, narcotics proceeds |
| FinCEN | BSA/AML | Money laundering, structuring, terrorist financing |
| OFAC | Sanctions | Transactions with SDN-listed parties, embargoed jurisdictions |
| PCAOB | Auditor oversight | Auditor independence, quality of financial statement audits |
| FINRA | Broker-dealers | Securities fraud, unsuitable investments |
| OCC | National banks | BSA compliance, financial crimes by bank employees |
| FDIC / Federal Reserve | State member banks | Similar to OCC |
| State AG offices | State-level | Consumer fraud, securities fraud, insurance fraud |

## International Standards

### FATF (Financial Action Task Force)
- 40 Recommendations for national AML/CFT frameworks
- Mutual evaluation process — countries rated on compliance
- FATF grey/black list for high-risk jurisdictions
- Key recommendations relevant to investigation: R.10 (CDD), R.20 (STR), R.24 (beneficial ownership)

### IFRS vs. US GAAP Key Differences (Forensic Relevance)
| Issue | US GAAP | IFRS |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue recognition | ASC 606 (5-step model) | IFRS 15 (similar 5-step) |
| Lease accounting | ASC 842 | IFRS 16 |
| Inventory | LIFO permitted | LIFO prohibited |
| Development costs | Expensed (generally) | Capitalized (if criteria met) |
| Revaluation of PP&E | Cost model only | Revaluation model permitted |

## Common Statutes Used in Financial Fraud Prosecutions

| Statute | Elements | Common Application |
|---|---|---|
| 18 USC 1341 (Mail Fraud) | Scheme to defraud + use of mail | Broad applicability in fraud schemes |
| 18 USC 1343 (Wire Fraud) | Scheme to defraud + use of wire communication | Extremely broad; most financial fraud |
| 18 USC 1344 (Bank Fraud) | Scheme to defraud financial institution | Loan fraud, check fraud, account takeover |
| 18 USC 1956 (Money Laundering) | Transaction with SUA proceeds + concealment or promotion | Layering, integration of fraud proceeds |
| 18 USC 1957 (Engaging in monetary transactions) | >$10K transaction with criminal proceeds | Less intent required than §1956 |
| 15 USC 78ff (Securities Fraud) | Material misstatement + scienter + in connection with securities | Public company financial fraud |
| 26 USC 7201 (Tax Evasion) | Willful attempt to evade tax | Unreported income from fraud schemes |
