# Fraud Scheme Catalog

A reference catalog of common occupational fraud schemes, organized by the ACFE Fraud Tree, with detection indicators for each.

---

## Asset Misappropriation

### Cash Schemes

#### Skimming
**Definition**: Theft of cash before it's recorded in the accounting system
**Indicators**:
- Cash register shortages without explanation
- Voided transactions that don't match actual voids in system
- Missing receipts for cash transactions
- Revenue trends inconsistent with non-cash transaction growth
- Employees with financial difficulties handling cash

#### Cash Larceny
**Definition**: Theft of cash after it has been recorded
**Indicators**:
- Deposits in transit that never clear
- Bank balance lower than book balance (unexplained)
- Frequent adjustments to cash accounts
- Single person controlling receipts, posting, and reconciliation (SoD failure)

#### Payroll Fraud (Ghost Employees)
**Definition**: Adding fictitious employees or retaining terminated employees on payroll
**Indicators**:
- Employees with no HR record, benefits enrollment, or supervisor
- Multiple employees sharing bank account routing/account numbers
- Employees sharing address with other employees or vendors
- Payroll payments to terminated employees after HR termination date
- Employee ID numbers not matching HR system sequence

#### Payroll Fraud (Falsified Hours/Commissions)
**Indicators**:
- Overtime clustered with specific supervisors
- Commission amounts inconsistent with documented sales
- Manual timesheet adjustments without approval trail

### Billing Schemes

#### Fictitious Vendor
**Definition**: Fraudster creates a fake vendor and submits invoices for non-existent goods/services
**Indicators**:
- Vendor has PO Box only, no physical address
- Vendor registered at employee's home address (exact match)
- Vendor EIN matches employee SSN
- No web presence, phone, or verifiable contact
- Vendor added to master file shortly before first payment
- Invoices lack typical vendor branding (professional formatting, logo)
- Services described in vague terms ("consulting," "professional services")

#### Personal Purchases via Company Account
**Indicators**:
- Vendor is a retail store inconsistent with business purpose
- Item descriptions (electronics, clothing, home goods)
- Delivery address matches employee home
- Purchase timing (personal calendar dates, weekends, holidays)

#### Overbilling / Double Billing
**Indicators**:
- Invoices with no corresponding PO or receiving report
- Duplicate invoice numbers across different payment dates
- Invoice amounts in round numbers (no itemization)
- Price per unit inconsistent with market rates

### Check Tampering

#### Forged Maker
**Definition**: Unauthorized person signs company checks
**Indicators**:
- Checks clearing with signatures inconsistent with authorized signers
- Checks for amounts below dual-signature threshold (threshold avoidance)
- Checks made payable to individuals rather than vendors
- Check sequence out of order with other cleared checks

#### Altered Payee
**Definition**: Legitimate check intercepted and payee name changed
**Indicators**:
- Payee name on cleared check doesn't match check register
- Payee endorsements inconsistent with named payee
- Checks voided in system but with no corresponding void stamp on physical check

### Expense Reimbursement

#### Mischaracterized Expenses
**Indicators**:
- Personal purchases submitted as business expenses
- Entertainment claimed on days employee wasn't traveling for business
- Receipts for businesses inconsistent with stated purpose

#### Inflated Expenses
**Indicators**:
- Receipt amounts inconsistent with merchant average ticket
- Rounded amounts ($50.00 for a restaurant receipt)
- Same merchant appears on multiple employees' reports same date

#### Fictitious Expenses
**Indicators**:
- Receipts that are clearly altered (fonts, amounts)
- Duplicate receipts submitted across multiple reports
- Receipts for amounts exactly at policy maximum
- Missing receipts pattern for amounts just below receipt-required threshold

## Financial Statement Fraud

### Revenue Overstatement

#### Fictitious Revenue
**Indicators**:
- Revenue recognized with no corresponding cash receipt or valid receivable
- Customers who don't exist in any external database
- Revenue concentrated in final days of reporting period
- Round-number revenue entries with no invoice trail

#### Channel Stuffing
**Definition**: Pressuring distributors to take excess inventory, recording as revenue
**Indicators**:
- Receivables growing faster than revenue (rising DSO)
- High Q4 revenue followed by high Q1 returns/credits
- Side agreements with distributors (right of return, extended payment)
- Revenue reversal in subsequent period

#### Bill-and-Hold
**Definition**: Recording revenue for goods not yet shipped, held at seller's facility
**Indicators**:
- Footnote disclosure of bill-and-hold arrangements
- Inventory and revenue both higher than normal
- Customer not assuming risk of ownership on shipment date

### Expense Understatement

#### Capitalization of Operating Costs
**Definition**: Recording operating expenses as capital expenditures to improve income
**Indicators**:
- Unusual growth in intangible assets or deferred charges
- Capital expenditures growing without corresponding revenue/productivity growth
- Asset categories with vague descriptions
- WorldCom-style patterns: revenue targets met exactly by capitalizing line costs

#### Cookie Jar Reserves
**Definition**: Building excess reserves in good years, releasing them in bad years to smooth earnings
**Indicators**:
- Reserve accounts that track inversely with earnings pressure
- Reserve reversals in periods where company misses consensus estimates
- Restructuring charges taken and reversed across multiple periods

## Corruption

### Bid Rigging
**Indicators**:
- Single-source procurement for contracts that should be competitive
- Winning bid always from same vendor despite below-market pricing from others
- Vendor representatives with personal relationships to procurement staff
- Employee owns equity in or receives payments from winning vendor

### Kickbacks
**Indicators**:
- Vendor invoices higher than market rate
- Employee lifestyle inconsistent with compensation
- Procurement decisions that can't be justified on technical/price merit
- Employee fails to disclose vendor relationship on conflict-of-interest certification
