# Prompt: Post-Incident Safety Debrief

Structure a thorough safety debrief following a field incident or near-miss.

## Input Variables
- `{INCIDENT_TYPE}` — Person in water, equipment failure, weather event, exposure, etc.
- `{SEVERITY}` — Near-miss, minor, moderate, serious, critical
- `{INCIDENT_SUMMARY}` — Brief description of what happened
- `{TEAM_SIZE}` — Number of people involved
- `{OUTCOME}` — Injuries, equipment damage, data loss, etc.

## Prompt

Facilitate a structured post-incident debrief for a {SEVERITY} {INCIDENT_TYPE} incident during aquatic fieldwork. Summary: {INCIDENT_SUMMARY}. Team size: {TEAM_SIZE}. Outcome: {OUTCOME}.

Structure the debrief as follows:

1. **Timeline Reconstruction** — Walk through the sequence of events. What happened first? What decisions were made and when? Where were all team members at each point?

2. **What Went Right** — Start with positives. What safety measures worked? What good decisions were made? What training paid off?

3. **Root Cause Analysis** — Use the "5 Whys" method to dig past the surface cause. Was this a procedure failure, equipment failure, environmental factor, human factor, or organizational factor? Often it's a combination.

4. **Protocol Review** — Were existing safety protocols followed? If not, why not? Were the protocols adequate for the situation? Did anyone feel pressure (time, workload, social) to skip safety steps?

5. **Contributing Factors** — Fatigue, weather changes, equipment condition, communication gaps, experience level, complacency, time pressure

6. **Corrective Actions** — Specific, measurable, assigned, with deadlines:
   - Immediate (before next field day)
   - Short-term (within 30 days)
   - Long-term (policy/procedural changes)

7. **Lessons Learned** — What should every field team member know as a result of this incident? Draft a 2-3 sentence safety bulletin.

8. **Follow-up** — Who needs to be notified? What documentation is required? When will corrective actions be verified?

Maintain a blame-free tone throughout. The goal is systemic improvement, not individual fault-finding.
