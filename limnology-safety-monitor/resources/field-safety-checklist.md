# Field Safety Quick-Reference Checklist

## Pre-Departure (Office/Lab)

- [ ] Weather forecast reviewed — no hard no-go conditions
- [ ] Float plan filed with designated contact (site, personnel, expected return time)
- [ ] Emergency contacts confirmed and accessible
- [ ] All personnel briefed on site-specific hazards
- [ ] Equipment manifest verified against sampling plan
- [ ] Instruments calibrated and calibration logged
- [ ] Sample bottles labeled and packed with preservatives
- [ ] First aid kit stocked and sealed
- [ ] Communication devices charged (VHF radio, cell phone, PLB)
- [ ] Vehicle fueled, route confirmed

## Site Arrival

- [ ] Visual hazard scan of water body and access area
- [ ] Wind speed/direction check (handheld anemometer or visual indicators)
- [ ] Water conditions assessed (color, clarity, surface conditions, odor)
- [ ] Access path evaluated (footing, slope, obstacles)
- [ ] PFDs donned by all personnel within 3m of water
- [ ] Buddy pairs confirmed
- [ ] GPS coordinates logged for emergency reference
- [ ] Check-in with float plan contact — confirm arrival

## Before Launching Watercraft

- [ ] PFDs properly worn (not just accessible) by ALL occupants
- [ ] Drain plug installed
- [ ] Motor starts, kill switch functional
- [ ] Backup propulsion available (paddle/oar)
- [ ] Anchor and line ready
- [ ] All loose equipment secured
- [ ] Capacity not exceeded (persons + gear)
- [ ] Communication device accessible (not buried in gear bag)

## During Sampling (Hourly)

- [ ] Weather re-evaluation — any deterioration?
- [ ] All personnel accounted for and in good condition
- [ ] Hydration check (especially in heat)
- [ ] Equipment functioning properly
- [ ] Sample integrity maintained (cooler temperature, custody)
- [ ] Check-in with shore/float plan contact if scheduled

## Return to Shore

- [ ] All personnel accounted for
- [ ] All equipment recovered and secured
- [ ] Watercraft properly beached/trailered
- [ ] Post-field calibration check performed and logged
- [ ] Samples properly stored in cooler, chain of custody updated
- [ ] Site left clean (pack out everything)
- [ ] Float plan contact notified of safe return

## Post-Field (Office/Lab)

- [ ] Samples delivered to lab within holding times
- [ ] Chain of custody completed and signed
- [ ] Field notes reviewed and completed
- [ ] Calibration drift documented
- [ ] Any incidents or near-misses documented
- [ ] Equipment cleaned, inspected, and stored
- [ ] Data entered into database/spreadsheet

## Emergency Quick Reference

### Person in Water
1. **SHOUT** — Alert all personnel
2. **THROW** — Rope bag, PFD, anything buoyant
3. **DON'T GO** — Do NOT enter water without proper rescue training and equipment
4. **CALL** — 911 if victim cannot self-rescue

### Hypothermia Signs (water temp <15°C)
- Shivering → Confusion → Loss of coordination → Unconsciousness
- **Action:** Remove wet clothing, insulate, warm core (not extremities), evacuate

### Lightning
- If thunder heard or lightning seen: **Immediately leave the water**
- Seek shelter in vehicle or substantial building
- Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before returning to water
- **Small boats on open water are extremely vulnerable**

### HAB Exposure
- Skin contact: Wash with clean water and soap
- Eye contact: Flush with clean water for 15 minutes
- Ingestion: Do not induce vomiting, seek medical attention
- Inhalation (spray): Move to fresh air, seek medical attention if symptomatic

### Capsized Boat
1. Stay with the boat (it floats even when swamped)
2. Account for all personnel
3. Signal for help (whistle, radio, visual signals)
4. Attempt to right the boat only if safe and practical
5. If in cold water: assume Heat Escape Lessening Posture (HELP) — draw knees to chest, cross arms
