# Creation Report: Frequency Hopping Analysis Workspace

This workspace was generated on 2026-05-06 (Cognitropy day 42) from the seed `(RF/SDR/Signals, frequency hopping analysis, using Bayesian probability assessment)`. The creative pairing — FHSS reverse engineering with explicit Bayesian inference rather than the usual rule-of-thumb thresholds — exists because the standard SDR workflow (tune, demodulate, decode) silently fails on hopping signals: operators see noise, conclude the link is encrypted, and stop. The Bayesian framing is not decorative: hop-set / dwell / sequence estimation is fundamentally a problem of partial observation under uncertainty, and posteriors with credibility intervals are the only way to honestly report what was recovered. This workspace therefore takes a stance — every hop-rate estimate, channel-set claim, and sequence identification is reported as a posterior with prior and likelihood model, not a point estimate. Calibration on Bluetooth Classic (1600 hops/s, 79 channels, LAP-derived sequence) is mandatory before unknown captures get analysed.
