# Standards and References Guide

A curated list of essential standards, documents, and resources for railway signaling engineers. Organized by career stage.

---

## Essential for All Levels

**Network Rail Rule Book (GG/RT3451 series)**
- The operational rulebook governing train movements and signalling operations
- Available via Network Rail employee portal and published on Network Rail website

**Railway Group Standards (ROGS)**
- Published by Network Rail as the infrastructure manager
- Available via Network Rail SPARK portal (login required)

**IRSE Website — www.irse.org**
- Institution of Railway Signal Engineers
- Licensing scheme details, technical papers, training resources
- Good Signal newsletter archives — real-world case studies and news

## ETCS/ERTMS Resources (L4+)

**ERA ETCS Subsets (free download from era.europa.eu)**
- Subset-026: System Requirements Specification (the main ETCS spec)
- Subset-034: Train Interface specification
- Subset-035: Specific Transmission Module (STM) requirements
- Subset-091: Safety Requirements for the ETCS DMI

**ERTMS User Group — www.ertms.net**
- Industry news, deployment updates, technical resources

---

## Learning Resources

**IRSE Technical Notes:**
- Free from irse.org — covers specific topics in accessible technical depth
- Good bridge between NVQ knowledge and engineering-level understanding

**Railway Technology Magazines:**
- Railway Gazette International — industry news with technical depth
- Modern Railways — UK focus, good for infrastructure and signaling news

**YouTube Channels:**
- Various heritage railway and enthusiast channels show mechanical interlocking operation — useful for visualizing the physical principles before studying electronic versions

**RSSB (Rail Safety and Standards Board) — www.rssb.co.uk**
- Publishes safety data, accident investigation reports, and risk analysis
- Essential reading at L4+ for understanding the systemic safety picture

---

## Incident Investigation Reports

Free from gov.uk and rssb.co.uk:

- **RAIB (Rail Accident Investigation Branch)** — publishes all UK railway investigation reports
  - Search: raib.gov.uk
  - Every RAIB report is a free education in what went wrong and why
- **AIB (Accident Investigation Board)** — historical investigations pre-RAIB

Recommended reports for signaling learning:
1. RAIB Report on Ladbroke Grove / Paddington 1999 (HSE Cullen Inquiry)
2. RAIB Report on Potters Bar 2002
3. RAIB Report on Grayrigg 2007
4. RAIB Quarterly Safety Digests — short summaries of recent incidents

## Exam Preparation

**City & Guilds / EAL NVQ Level 3 Railway Engineering (Signalling)**
- Contact your training provider for specific unit guides
- Core units: Installing, Testing, Maintaining and Diagnosing Faults on Signalling Systems

**IRSE Technician Licence Guidance**
- irse.org/licensing — covers the interview format and evidence requirements

**Engineering Council — engc.org.uk**
- Routes to EngTech, IEng, CEng registration
- UK Standard for Professional Engineering Competence (UK-SPEC) — the benchmark document
