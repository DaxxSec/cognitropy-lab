# Getting Started with Signal Mentor

Welcome to the Railway Signaling Systems workspace. This guide gets you up and running in under 10 minutes.

## Step 1: Run `/onboard`

Type `/onboard` in your Claude chat. Signal Mentor will ask you a few questions about your background and learning goals, then configure the workspace for your level.

This takes about 5 minutes and makes every subsequent session much more useful.

## Step 2: Review Your Profile

After onboarding, check `context/role.md` — this is where your competency level, qualifications, and authorizations are stored. Correct anything that's wrong.

## Step 3: Start Learning

You have a few options depending on what you need:

- **Stuck on a concept?** → `/explain [topic]`
- **Preparing for an assessment?** → `/quiz` or `/quiz [topic]`
- **Want to see your progress?** → `/progress`
- **Learning from real incidents?** → `/incident [incident name]`

## Step 4: Keep the Session Log Updated

After each session, ask Signal Mentor to log what you covered: "Can you add today's session to the work log?"

Over time this builds a record of your learning that can feed into your NVQ evidence portfolio.

---

## Tips

- **Be specific about your level.** The agent calibrates everything to your documented level — if you want a simpler or more advanced explanation, just say so.
- **Use it for exam prep.** Run `/quiz` multiple times before assessments — each run generates fresh questions.
- **Incidents are goldmines.** The `/incident` command turns real accidents into structured learning. Use it regularly — it's one of the most effective ways to build deep understanding of why the rules exist.
- **Safety first, always.** If you describe a real live scenario and you're not sure what to do — the answer is always: stop, escalate, don't proceed. Signal Mentor will reinforce this.
