# Domain Knowledge: Curriculum Design & Learning Objectives

## Bloom's Revised Taxonomy (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001)

The cognitive process dimension has six levels, each with associated measurable verbs:

### 1. Remember (Retrieve knowledge)
Verbs: define, list, recall, recognize, identify, name, state, match, label

### 2. Understand (Construct meaning)
Verbs: explain, summarize, paraphrase, classify, compare, interpret, exemplify, infer

### 3. Apply (Use in new situations)
Verbs: execute, implement, solve, demonstrate, use, calculate, apply, construct

### 4. Analyze (Break into parts, find relationships)
Verbs: differentiate, organize, attribute, deconstruct, compare, contrast, distinguish, examine

### 5. Evaluate (Make judgments)
Verbs: critique, judge, justify, assess, argue, defend, appraise, evaluate, prioritize

### 6. Create (Produce original work)
Verbs: design, construct, produce, develop, formulate, compose, plan, invent, generate

### Knowledge Dimensions (cross-reference with cognitive process)
- **Factual:** Terminology, specific details and elements
- **Conceptual:** Classifications, principles, theories, models
- **Procedural:** Skills, algorithms, techniques, methods
- **Metacognitive:** Self-knowledge, strategic knowledge, cognitive tasks

## Backward Design (Understanding by Design)

### Stage 1: Desired Results
- Establish goals (standards, transfer goals)
- Identify enduring understandings (big ideas)
- Write essential questions (open-ended, thought-provoking)
- Specify knowledge and skills students will acquire

### Stage 2: Assessment Evidence
- Performance tasks (authentic application)
- Other evidence (quizzes, observations, homework, journals)
- Student self-assessment and reflection

### Stage 3: Learning Plan
- Activities sequenced to build toward understanding
- WHERE TO framework:
  - **W**here is the unit going? What is expected?
  - **H**ook the students. Hold their interest.
  - **E**quip students with tools, experience, knowledge
  - **R**ethink and revise understandings
  - **E**valuate results and progress
  - **T**ailor to individual needs
  - **O**rganize for deep understanding

## Webb's Depth of Knowledge (DOK)

### DOK Level 1: Recall and Reproduction
- Recall a fact, definition, or procedure
- Simple one-step operations

### DOK Level 2: Skills and Concepts
- Requires mental processing beyond recall
- Classify, organize, compare, estimate, interpret data

### DOK Level 3: Strategic Thinking
- Requires reasoning, planning, evidence
- Develop a logical argument, explain phenomena, draw conclusions

### DOK Level 4: Extended Thinking
- Requires investigation, complex reasoning, time
- Design an experiment, synthesize across sources, create a model

## Quality Matters (QM) Essential Standards (for online/hybrid courses)

1. Course overview and introduction set clear expectations
2. Learning objectives are measurable and consistent with activities/assessments
3. Assessment strategies measure stated objectives
4. Instructional materials support learning objectives
5. Learning activities promote achievement of objectives
6. Course technology supports learner engagement
7. Learner support information is clear
8. Accessibility and usability standards are met

## Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Principles

### Engagement (the "Why" of learning)
- Provide options for recruiting interest
- Provide options for sustaining effort and persistence
- Provide options for self-regulation

### Representation (the "What" of learning)
- Provide options for perception
- Provide options for language and symbols
- Provide options for comprehension

### Action & Expression (the "How" of learning)
- Provide options for physical action
- Provide options for expression and communication
- Provide options for executive functions
