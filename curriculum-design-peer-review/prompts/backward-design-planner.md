# Backward Design Planner

Plan a curriculum unit using the Understanding by Design (UbD) framework.

## Prompt

Design a unit plan for:
- **Subject:** [subject area]
- **Topic:** [specific topic]
- **Duration:** [timeframe]
- **Audience:** [grade level / learner profile]
- **Standards:** [applicable framework]

Follow the three stages of backward design:

### Stage 1: Desired Results
- **Transfer Goals:** What will students be able to do independently after this unit?
- **Enduring Understandings:** What are the big ideas that have lasting value?
- **Essential Questions:** What open-ended questions will guide inquiry?
- **Knowledge:** What key facts and concepts will students learn?
- **Skills:** What processes and procedures will they master?

### Stage 2: Assessment Evidence
- **Performance Task:** Design one authentic task where students demonstrate transfer
- **Other Evidence:** List formative and summative checks for each objective
- **Self-Assessment:** How will students reflect on their own learning?

### Stage 3: Learning Plan
- **Daily/Session Breakdown:** Sequence activities across the unit
- **WHERE TO Elements:** Tag each activity with its WHERE TO function
- **Differentiation:** Note UDL accommodations for diverse learners
- **Materials Needed:** List all resources and materials
