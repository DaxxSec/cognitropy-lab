# Peer Review Checklist for Curriculum Materials

Use this checklist when running the `/peer-review` command or conducting manual reviews.

## 1. Learning Objectives Quality
- [ ] Each objective uses a measurable verb from Bloom's Revised Taxonomy
- [ ] No vague verbs (understand, know, learn, appreciate, be aware of)
- [ ] Each objective specifies what the student will do, with what content, and under what conditions
- [ ] Objectives progress from lower to higher cognitive complexity
- [ ] Both knowledge dimensions and process dimensions are addressed

## 2. Alignment (Objectives ↔ Assessments ↔ Activities)
- [ ] Every objective has at least one corresponding assessment
- [ ] Every assessment maps to at least one objective
- [ ] Activities are designed to build the skills/knowledge needed for assessments
- [ ] No orphaned assessments (assessing something not in the objectives)
- [ ] No orphaned activities (practicing something never assessed)

## 3. Standards Mapping
- [ ] Objectives are mapped to applicable standards framework(s)
- [ ] All required standards for the unit/course are addressed
- [ ] Standards codes or identifiers are cited correctly
- [ ] Gap analysis has been performed

## 4. Rigor and Cognitive Complexity
- [ ] Bloom's levels are distributed across the unit (not all at Remember/Understand)
- [ ] DOK levels include Level 3+ tasks for at least 30% of objectives
- [ ] Higher-order objectives have sufficient scaffolding from earlier activities
- [ ] The culminating assessment requires transfer or creation

## 5. Assessment Design
- [ ] Formative assessments are spaced throughout the unit
- [ ] At least one authentic performance task exists
- [ ] Rubrics are provided for subjective assessments
- [ ] Rubric criteria align directly with objective verbs
- [ ] Multiple assessment types are used (not all tests/quizzes)

## 6. Inclusivity and Accessibility (UDL)
- [ ] Multiple means of engagement are offered
- [ ] Multiple means of representation are present
- [ ] Multiple means of action/expression are available
- [ ] Examples and scenarios are culturally responsive
- [ ] Materials are accessible (screen reader compatible, captioned, etc.)
- [ ] Accommodations and modifications are noted

## 7. Structure and Clarity
- [ ] Unit plan follows a logical sequence
- [ ] Time estimates are realistic for the content
- [ ] Materials and resources are listed
- [ ] Prerequisites and prior knowledge are stated
- [ ] Vocabulary and key terms are identified
