# Webb's Depth of Knowledge (DOK) Guide

Use DOK alongside Bloom's Taxonomy to evaluate the cognitive complexity of tasks.

## DOK Level 1: Recall and Reproduction
**Complexity:** Low — one correct answer, one step
**Student actions:** recall, recognize, identify, define, list, state, name
**Assessment examples:** multiple choice (factual), fill-in-the-blank, label a diagram, match terms
**Curriculum note:** Necessary as foundation, but units should not stay here

## DOK Level 2: Skills and Concepts
**Complexity:** Moderate — requires some mental processing beyond recall
**Student actions:** classify, organize, estimate, compare, summarize, interpret, predict, observe, collect, display
**Assessment examples:** short answer requiring explanation, data tables, graphic organizers, concept maps
**Curriculum note:** Good for building procedural knowledge

## DOK Level 3: Strategic Thinking
**Complexity:** High — requires reasoning, planning, citing evidence, multi-step problem solving
**Student actions:** construct, revise, critique, investigate, assess, develop a logical argument, cite evidence, draw conclusions, differentiate
**Assessment examples:** essays with evidence, research reports, experimental design, case study analysis
**Curriculum note:** Target level for most units — demonstrates transfer

## DOK Level 4: Extended Thinking
**Complexity:** Very high — requires investigation, time, synthesis across sources/domains
**Student actions:** design, connect, synthesize, apply concepts to new situations, create, critique across sources, plan and conduct research
**Assessment examples:** long-term projects, original research, portfolio with reflection, interdisciplinary capstone
**Curriculum note:** Appropriate for culminating performances and advanced courses

## DOK vs. Bloom's
DOK is about **complexity of thinking** required, not just the cognitive process.
- A Bloom's "Create" task at DOK 1: "Create a list of vocabulary words"
- A Bloom's "Create" task at DOK 4: "Design and conduct an original experiment"

Use both frameworks together for accurate rigor assessment.
