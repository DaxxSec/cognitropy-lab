# SEAD 4 — 13 Adjudicative Guidelines (reference summary)

> Security Executive Agent Directive 4, "National Security Adjudicative Guidelines," 2017.
> This file is a condensed reference; the authoritative text is the SEAD 4 document itself.

Each guideline has:
- A concern statement.
- Enumerated **disqualifying conditions** (DC).
- Enumerated **mitigating conditions** (MC).

The adjudicator applies the **whole-person concept** to weigh the totality.

---

## Guideline A — Allegiance to the United States
- DC: involvement in activities, membership in organizations, or expressions advocating overthrow of the government.
- MC: involvement was minimal, remote in time, or under duress.

## Guideline B — Foreign Influence
- DC: foreign contacts creating heightened risk of foreign exploitation or coercion; foreign financial interests.
- MC: relationship is so minimal infrequent as to create no risk; subject reports contacts; relocation of foreign family obligations.

## Guideline C — Foreign Preference
- DC: exercise of non-U.S. citizenship; acceptance of foreign benefits where a reasonable person would perceive preference.
- MC: dual citizenship is based solely on parents' birth country and has not been exercised; willingness to renounce.

## Guideline D — Sexual Behavior
- DC: of a criminal nature, involving lack of discretion or judgment, reflecting lack of judgment that could expose person to coercion.
- MC: long ago, not recent; occurred during adolescence; no longer a basis for coercion.

## Guideline E — Personal Conduct
- DC: untrustworthy or unreliable behavior, concealment, deliberate omission from security forms, pattern of dishonesty.
- MC: falsification corrected before confrontation; minor offense; conduct unlikely to recur.

## Guideline F — Financial Considerations
- DC: inability to satisfy debts; history of not meeting obligations; deceptive financial practices; unexplained affluence.
- MC: behavior happened long ago under unusual circumstances; financial counseling and progress; initiative to resolve.

## Guideline G — Alcohol Consumption
- DC: alcohol-related incidents, habitual or binge consumption to impairment, diagnosis by medical professional.
- MC: so infrequent/so long ago; completed treatment; abstinent for period recommended by medical professional.

## Guideline H — Drug Involvement and Substance Misuse
- DC: illegal use; possession; cultivation; failed drug test; diagnosis of substance use disorder.
- MC: not recent; abstinence with demonstrated intent not to use; completed treatment; prescription per physician.

## Guideline I — Psychological Conditions
- DC: condition that may impair judgment, reliability, trustworthiness; failure to follow treatment plan.
- MC: condition controlled by ongoing treatment; recovered; condition does not impair duty.

## Guideline J — Criminal Conduct
- DC: allegation or admission of criminal conduct; multiple lesser offenses; pattern of disregard for law.
- MC: so much time has passed; pressures no longer exist; rehabilitation.

## Guideline K — Handling Protected Information
- DC: deliberate or negligent disclosure; pattern of violations; collecting or storing protected info in unauthorized locations.
- MC: inadvertent; took responsibility; training and security awareness obtained.

## Guideline L — Outside Activities
- DC: involvement with a foreign entity or organization that could create a conflict of interest with national security duties.
- MC: activity ceased; activity did not involve protected information; authorization obtained.

## Guideline M — Use of Information Technology
- DC: unauthorized entry/modification of systems; introduction of malicious code; circumvention of controls.
- MC: incident was inadvertent; obsolete circumstances; affirmative steps to prevent recurrence.

---

## Whole-Person Factors (applied to every case)
1. Nature, extent, and seriousness of the conduct
2. Circumstances surrounding the conduct
3. Frequency and recency
4. Individual's age and maturity at the time of conduct
5. Extent of voluntary participation
6. Presence or absence of rehabilitation and other permanent behavioral changes
7. Motivation for the conduct
8. Potential for pressure, coercion, exploitation, or duress
9. Likelihood of continuation or recurrence

The whole-person concept is mandatory. A single disqualifying condition does not automatically result in denial; a single mitigating condition does not automatically result in favorable adjudication. The adjudicator weighs all factors.
