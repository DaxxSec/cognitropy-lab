# Onboard — Workspace Initialization

Welcome to the Hydraulic Engineering Fluid Dynamics workspace. I'll gather the information needed to configure this workspace for your project.

## Interview Flow

### 1. Project Definition
- What type of hydraulic system are you working with? (open channel, pressurized pipe network, combined sewer, dam/reservoir, stormwater, other)
- What is the geographic scope? (watershed, municipality, specific facility)
- What are the primary deliverables? (risk assessment, design calculations, flood study, master plan)

### 2. Your Role
- What is your title/role?
- What is your experience level with hydraulic engineering?
- What is your experience level with probabilistic/Bayesian methods?
- What do you need this agent to help with most?

### 3. Data Sources
- What flow/stage data do you have access to? (USGS gauges, SCADA, flow monitors, survey data)
- What asset data do you have? (GIS, CAD, spreadsheets, database)
- What terrain/spatial data? (LiDAR, contours, NHDPlus)

### 4. Regulatory Context
- What design standards apply? (FEMA, state stormwater manual, dam safety rules, AWWA)
- Are there specific return periods or design criteria you must meet?

### 5. Computational Environment
- What OS do you run?
- What hydraulic software do you have installed? (HEC-RAS, EPANET, SWMM, HEC-HMS)
- Python version and package manager?

### 6. Constraints
- Any data sensitivity concerns? (critical infrastructure, PCII)
- Computational limitations? (RAM, CPU cores)
- Timeline constraints?

## After Interview
I will populate `context/project.md`, `context/role.md`, `context/constraints.md`, and `context/for-agent/environment.md` with your responses, then recommend which commands to start with.
