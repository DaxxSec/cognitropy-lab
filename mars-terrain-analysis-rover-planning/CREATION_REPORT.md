## Why this workspace

The day-41 Cognitropy assignment fused Mars terrain analysis rover planning with the analytical framework of jazz harmony, executed via peer review workflows. Three things had to fit together cleanly: the terrain/mobility substrate had to be real-mission accurate (per-rover slope/tilt/rock limits, real datasets — HiRISE, CTX, MOLA), the borrowed framework had to be a sharp thinking tool rather than a poetry exercise (chords as waypoint property triads, cadences as resolution-to-safe-parking, substitutions as concrete planning moves), and the peer review workflow had to be operationally tight (5-role panel, scored rubric with hard-fail authority, dissent captured verbatim, decision log as canonical "why was this approved" artifact). The result is a workspace where reviewers can argue about whether a candidate is "a tritone substitution that buys 3 m of clearance at the cost of 8% drive time" — a sentence that maps a jazz-theory move onto a real Mars-rover trade-off — and have that conversation be more precise, not less, than the equivalent in plain rover-driver vocabulary.
