# post-trip-aar

## Purpose

Use within 72 hours of trip conclusion to run the structured After Action Review. Surfaces what worked, what didn't, and what protocol revisions should follow.

## Prompt Template

```
Acting as the advance planner / RSO leading the post-trip AAR session.

AAR context:

- **Trip identification:** [protectee tier, jurisdictions, dates]
- **Pre-trip artifacts location:** [path to advance plan + venue surveys + recons + configs + contingency tree]
- **In-trip deviation log location:** [path]
- **Detail team roster:** [list of agents, drivers, CAT lead, medical, intel support]
- **Notable in-trip events:** [incidents, close calls, near-misses, complaints, protocol activations]
- **Threat-stream summary during trip:** [tier shifts, surprise reporting, source-silence periods]
- **Outcomes:** [protectee transit completion, family transit, no-incident vs. incident-managed]

Please:
1. Walk through trip chronologically; per increment, surface what was planned vs. what happened.
2. Per increment, ask the canonical AAR questions: planned / actual / went well / didn't / revision proposal.
3. Identify pattern findings across increments — recurring issues at venue type, jurisdiction, time of day, fitter.
4. For each pattern, draft a specific actionable protocol revision proposal.
5. Identify training-need findings — specific to individual team members or to the team collectively.
6. Identify equipment-need findings — what gear was missing or inadequate.
7. Document deviations from protocol — intentional + inadvertent — with rationale and outcome.
8. Surface any near-misses or close calls; ensure they're documented even if no incident resulted.
9. Generate the AAR memo with sign-off block (Detail Leader + RSO).
10. Propose escalation for any pattern findings requiring DMAIC-equivalent process improvement work.
```

## Expected Output

- Per-increment AAR findings (planned / actual / went well / didn't / revision)
- Pattern findings across increments
- Training + equipment need findings
- Deviation log analysis
- Near-miss documentation
- Protocol revision proposals (specific, actionable, accountable)
- AAR memo with sign-off block
- Escalation proposals for systemic findings

## Notes

- AAR is candid by design — protect openness by separating from performance-review channels.
- Pattern findings matter more than per-incident findings — track across trips.
- Within 72 hours is non-negotiable; memory fades fast, and inter-trip transitions blur findings.
- Protocol revisions must be specific, actionable, and have a named owner — vague revisions don't get implemented.
- Document EVERY deviation, even minor ones; the pattern across trips is more informative than any individual incident.
