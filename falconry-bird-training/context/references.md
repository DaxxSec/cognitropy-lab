# Falconry Bird Training — References

Lookup data the agent reaches for during permit work, training, and stakeholder communications. Compact by design — defer to your **state agency** for binding specifics; rules vary and change.

## U.S. permit classes (federal baseline — states may be stricter)

| Class | Prerequisite | Birds possessed | Sponsor? | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| Apprentice | Age min (commonly 12–14), pass exam, facility/equipment inspection | 1 | Required (General/Master) | Minimum 2 years; restricted species |
| General | ≥2 years as Apprentice | Up to 3 | No | Broader species access |
| Master | ≥5 years as General | Up to 5 wild (more captive-bred under many readings) | No | May sponsor; eligible for eagles w/ extra permit |

## Species commonly permitted to apprentices

| Species | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Red-tailed hawk (*Buteo jamaicensis*) | Classic apprentice bird; passage birds preferred; hardy |
| American kestrel (*Falco sparverius*) | Small, low margin on weight; precise scale work essential |
| Red-shouldered hawk (*Buteo lineatus*) | Allowed in some states |

> Harris's hawk, peregrine, goshawk, and other species are generally **General/Master**-class and/or captive-bred only. Always confirm with the state — apprentice species lists vary.

## Key regulations and forms (U.S.)

| Item | What it is |
|------|-----------|
| Migratory Bird Treaty Act (MBTA) | The statute protecting native migratory birds, incl. raptors |
| 50 CFR 21.82 | Federal falconry standards & permitting (moved from §21.29 in the 2022 Part 21 reorg) |
| State falconry regulations | The binding day-to-day rules; administered by each state's wildlife agency |
| USFWS Form 3-186A | Migratory Bird Acquisition/Disposition/Banding report — file on acquisition, transfer, loss, or rebanding (commonly within 10 days; many states now collect this electronically) |
| Capture/take authorization | State-issued; governs trapping passage/eyass birds, seasons, and limits |
| Abatement / commercial endorsement | Permit add-on required before doing paid bird-abatement work |

## Equipment quick list

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Aylmeri anklets + jesses (field/mews) | Leg attachment; field jesses slit-less to avoid snagging |
| Swivel + leash | Safe tethering to perch |
| Hood | Calms bird via visual quiet; transport |
| Bells + radio telemetry | Location; telemetry effectively mandatory for free flight |
| Bow perch / block perch | Hawks / falcons respectively; padded for foot health |
| Mews + weathering area | Indoor housing + outdoor sheltered area (inspection-regulated) |
| Gram scale | The weight log runs on it |
| Lure, glove, hawking bag, creance, bath pan | Training and daily care |

## Ailment quick table (sign → likely condition → urgency)

| Sign | Leading differential | Urgency |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| Cheesy mouth/crop lesions after pigeon/dove feeding | Frounce (trichomoniasis) | High — vet |
| Labored breathing, voice change, after damp/dirty housing or stress | Aspergillosis | High — vet |
| Swollen/hot foot, lameness, scabbed pad | Bumblefoot (pododermatitis) | Medium–High |
| Unplanned weight loss, fluffed, reluctant | "Going light" — disease until proven otherwise | High — vet, do not fly |
| Slow/full crop that won't pass | Sour/slow crop | Medium–High — vet |
| Poor mutes, weight loss, off food | Parasites (*Capillaria*, coccidia) | Medium — vet/fecal |

## Weight-management heuristics

- Discover flying weight **empirically** per bird; never read it off a chart.
- Track response **against** weight daily — readiness = response at a safe weight, not low weight.
- A prominent, sharp keel on palpation = low condition; pair scale + body-condition scoring.
- "Going light" is a vet event, not a training state. Stop flying, run `/vet-intake`.

## Organizations and learning resources

- **NAFA — North American Falconers Association** — https://www.n-a-f-a.com/ — principal U.S. org (est. 1961); journal, meets, conservation.
- **IAF — International Association for Falconry** — https://www.iaf.org/ — global federation; falconry is UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.
- **The Modern Apprentice** — https://www.themodernapprentice.com/ — free apprentice-focused education (equipment, manning, weight).
- **The Archives of Falconry (The Peregrine Fund)** — https://peregrinefund.org/archives-of-falconry — history & reference, Boise, ID.
- **Association of Avian Veterinarians** — https://www.aav.org/ — locate an avian vet.
- **State falconry clubs** — most states have one; the source for local mentors, sponsors, and meets.

## Standard texts

- Beebe & Webster — *North American Falconry and Hunting Hawks* (the standard apprentice manual).
- Jack Mavrogordato — *A Hawk for the Bush*; *A Falcon in the Field* (classic husbandry/training).
- Tom Cade & others — *The Peregrine Falcon* (species reference, conservation context).
