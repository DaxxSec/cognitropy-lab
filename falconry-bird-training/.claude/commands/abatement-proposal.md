# /abatement-proposal

Draft a commercial bird-abatement service proposal and scope of work for a client site flown by trained raptors.

## Inputs

- Client and site type: vineyard, airfield, landfill, feedlot, food-processing, agriculture.
- Target/pest species (starlings, gulls, pigeons, blackbirds) and the asset being protected.
- The risk window (e.g. vineyard veraison-to-harvest), operating hours, and site safety constraints.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the abatement/commercial endorsement is on the permit** before drafting anything.
2. Scope the problem: target species, the asset's value, and when the damage window opens/closes.
3. Confirm the work is permissible — flag any protected-species or sensitive-habitat issues for the client to clear.
4. Design the program: number/type of birds, sortie pattern, days/hours, ramp-up and steady-state.
5. Define deliverables and cadence — per-shift `/abatement-report`, monthly efficacy summary.
6. Format the pitch with `prompts/abatement-client-pitch.md`; include pricing structure and term.

## Output

`outputs/abatement-proposal-<client>-YYYY-MM-DD.md`: problem statement, scope of work, program design (birds/sorties/schedule), deliverables & reporting cadence, term, pricing structure, and permit/compliance notes.

## Notes

- Sell honestly: dispersal manages pressure, it doesn't promise zero birds.
- Match the program to the asset's risk window — over-scoping wastes the client's money and your birds.
