# /landowner-access

Draft a hunting-access request and a simple stewardship agreement for a landowner whose ground you want to hawk.

## Inputs

- The property (location, rough acreage, boundaries) and the quarry it holds (rabbit, squirrel, game birds).
- Season/dates you'd hunt and rough frequency.
- Who controls access (owner vs. tenant) and any known sensitivities.

## Steps

1. Identify the right decision-maker — owner vs. tenant matters for valid permission.
2. Draft the request from `prompts/landowner-access-request.md`: who you are, what falconry is (low-impact, **no firearms**), dates, and courtesy.
3. Offer reciprocal value where genuine (rabbit/squirrel control benefits many landowners).
4. Include a liability acknowledgment and a request for written permission.
5. Record agreed boundaries, no-go zones, gates/parking, and any livestock/dog notes.
6. Schedule an end-of-season thank-you with a brief account of how it went.

## Output

`outputs/landowner-<name>-YYYY-MM-DD.md`: the access request letter, a one-page permission/stewardship record (boundaries, dates, quarry, conditions), and a reminder for the end-of-season follow-up.

## Notes

- Get it in writing — verbal-only permission evaporates the day a neighbor complains.
- Falconry's low-impact, no-firearm nature is the strongest selling point with wary landowners.
