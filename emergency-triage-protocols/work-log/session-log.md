# Session Log — Exercise & Event Documentation

**Purpose:** Track all triage exercises, drills, real incidents, and lessons learned for continuous improvement and competency documentation.

## How to Use This Log

1. **After each exercise or incident**, add a new entry using the template below
2. **Document objectively:** What happened, not opinions
3. **Analyze root causes:** Why did issues occur?
4. **Assign actions:** Who will address each improvement?
5. **Track status:** Review monthly; update on progress
6. **Maintain records:** Keep permanent for compliance and trend analysis

---

### SCENARIO / INCIDENT DETAILS
- Date & Time: 2026-03-15, 2:35 PM

- Type: 4-vehicle collision with construction material spill
- Duration: Initial triage 40 minutes; ED surge 3 hours total

### PATIENTS & TRIAGE
- Total Patients: 18
  - Immediate (Red): 4
  - Delayed (Yellow): 8
  - Minor (Green): 6
  - Expectant (Black): 0
- Triage Accuracy: 16/18 = 89%
- Average Triage Time: 2.2 minutes per patient
- Critical Assessment: 2 misclassifications
  - Patient #7: Altered mental status initially missed; should have been Immediate
  - Patient #12: Rib fracture severity underestimated; should have been Immediate

### RESOURCE UTILIZATION
- Staffing Surge: Phase 1 activated immediately; Phase 2 by 3:15 PM
- Staff Deployed: Baseline 8 RNs + 4 called in (total 12 RNs); 2 physicians; 5 techs
- Equipment Used: All monitors functional; no equipment failures
- Supply Consumption: Used 24 IV lines (adequate stock); IV fluids consumed moderately
- Transport: 4 ambulances sufficient; 1 diverted to trauma center

### COMMUNICATION & COORDINATION
- Incident Command: Clear and effective; ED director provided clear direction
- Triage Area: Setup completed in 5 minutes; well-organized
- Treatment Areas: Good flow; coordination between triage and treatment
- External Coordination: Good communication with EMS; timely mutual aid notification

### STRENGTHS OBSERVED
✓ Rapid incident recognition and surge activation
✓ Excellent triage area setup and organization
✓ Clear incident command structure
✓ Good communication and team coordination
✓ Effective resource allocation
✓ Fast triage time (2.2 min/patient) without sacrificing accuracy

### ISSUES & CHALLENGES IDENTIFIED

#### Issue #1: Mental Status Assessment Errors
- **Description:** Two patients misclassified; both had altered mental status that was initially missed
- **Root Cause:** Rushed assessment under time pressure; did not consistently apply START Step 3 (mental status assessment)
- **Impact:** Potential delay in care for 2 Immediate patients; risk of under-triage
- **Action:** Conduct focused protocol training on START Step 3; reinforce that mental status is a critical discriminator; emphasize systematic assessment despite time pressure
- **Owner:** Training Coordinator
- **Target Date:** 2026-04-15
- **Status:** To do

#### Issue #2: Supply Chain Communication Delay
- **Description:** 15-minute delay in securing additional IV fluids; supply chain not alerted to surge protocol activation
- **Root Cause:** Supply chain not integrated into surge activation notification system
- **Impact:** Potential supply shortage if incident had been larger or prolonged
- **Action:** Update surge protocol to include automatic notification to supply chain at Phase 1 activation; provide supply chain contact information to ED staff
- **Owner:** ED Director + Operations
- **Target Date:** 2026-04-01
- **Status:** To do

### LESSONS LEARNED
- Surge protocols work well for 15–20 patient incidents
- Triage accuracy is good (89%) but mental status assessment needs improvement
- Team coordination and communication were strong; no major breakdowns
- Supply chain integration needs attention for larger incidents
- Early escalation to Phase 2 may have been beneficial (consider at 70% vs. 75% capacity trigger)

### COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT
N/A (real incident, not training exercise)
