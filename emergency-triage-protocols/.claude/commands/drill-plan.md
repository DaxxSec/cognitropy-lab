# /drill-plan — Tabletop & Full-Scale Exercise Design

## Purpose
Design a tabletop discussion or full-scale exercise for training staff, assessing competency, and testing emergency plans. Supports drill planning, scenario development, and evaluation.

## When to Use
- Scheduling annual or quarterly training exercises
- Planning tabletop discussions on specific scenarios
- Designing full-scale drills with realistic patient simulation
- Testing protocol updates or new procedures
- Conducting competency assessments
- Evaluating surge response readiness

## Duration
- **Tabletop design:** 1–2 hours planning, 2–3 hours execution
- **Full-scale design:** 2–4 hours planning, 4–8 hours execution

## Input Required

I'll request:

### 1. Exercise Objectives
- What do you want participants to learn or demonstrate?
- Examples:
  - "Practice START triage protocol application"
  - "Test surge activation procedures"
  - "Evaluate communication under stress"
  - "Train new staff on MCI response"
  - "Validate equipment maintenance readiness"

### 2. Exercise Type
- **Tabletop:** Seated, discussion-based, scenario injection
- **Functional:** Simulate specific operational functions (triage setup, transport)
- **Full-scale:** Complete MCI simulation with actors, equipment, transport

### 3. Scenario Parameters
- **Incident type:** (vehicle crash, building collapse, weather, disease outbreak, etc.)
- **Estimated casualties:** (10–100+ patients)
- **Acuity mix:** (% Immediate, Delayed, Minor)
- **Special considerations:** (pediatric, hazmat exposure, multiple facilities, etc.)

### 4. Participants & Roles
- **Expected attendance:** (# staff from each department)
- **Key roles:** (ED director, triage lead, trauma surgeon, ops coordinator, etc.)
- **Observers:** (Quality improvement, administration, external evaluators?)

### 5. Facility Details
- **ED capacity & layout:** (# beds, triage area, trauma resus, etc.)
- **Available resources:** (equipment, supplies, staff)
- **Known constraints:** (Limited ICU beds? Staffing challenges? Equipment gaps?)

### 6. Learning Focus
- **Protocol focus:** (START, SALT, ESI, facility-specific)
- **Competency goals:** (Triage accuracy, communication, decision-making, timing)
- **Improvement areas:** (Based on previous exercises or incidents)

### 7. Timeline & Logistics
- **Preferred date(s):** (When to schedule?)
- **Duration:** (How long for exercise?)
- **Location:** (ED proper, simulation lab, off-site facility?)
- **Budget/Resources:** (Available for actor recruitment, supplies, etc.)

## How I Design Your Drill

### Step 1: Define Learning Objectives
I'll clarify what participants should learn or demonstrate:
- "By end of exercise, staff will: (1) correctly apply START triage to 15+ patients, (2) communicate decisions clearly, (3) escalate surge appropriately"

### Step 2: Develop Scenario
Using `prompts/mci-scenario-generator.md`:
- Realistic incident with specific details (location, time, number of patients)
- Patient roster with demographics, injuries, vital signs
- Injects and timeline (additional information revealed during exercise)
- Realistic complications (equipment failure, staff unavailable, supply shortage, etc.)

### Step 3: Design Exercise Flow

**For Tabletop:**
1. Opening briefing (5 min): Incident overview, objectives
2. Scenario presentation (10 min): Initial incident details
3. Discussion & decision-making (60–90 min): Facilitator guides Q&A, injects new info
4. Debrief (30–45 min): Learning discussion using "What/So What/Now What" framework

**For Full-Scale:**
1. Pre-exercise briefing (30 min): Safety, objectives, roles, logistics
2. Exercise start (120+ min): Realistic patient flow, triage setup, treatment
3. Injects (throughout): Escalation, complications, new arrivals
4. Exercise end (call at designated time or when scenario complete)
5. Debrief (60–90 min): Learning discussion, feedback

### Step 4: Develop Materials
- Scenario cards (patient presentations, vital signs)
- Facilitator guide (expected decisions, key learning points)
- Observer forms (triage accuracy, communication, timing)
- Role assignments (if needed)
- Supplies checklist (for full-scale: triage tags, bandages, supplies, etc.)

### Step 5: Plan Logistics
- **Venue:** ED proper, simulation lab, or conference room
- **Equipment:** Projector/screen (for tabletop), patient simulators/actors (for full-scale)
- **Supplies:** Triage tags, bandages, airway equipment, dressings, etc.
- **Staffing:** Facilitators, observers, safety officer
- **Time & date:** Scheduled avoiding peak ED operations if possible

### Step 6: Create Evaluation Plan
- **Success metrics:** Triage accuracy %, average time per patient, communication quality
- **Observer forms:** For tracking performance and feedback
- **Competency scoring:** If assessing individual competency
- **Data collection:** How will results be documented and analyzed?

## Example Drill Plan

```
EMERGENCY TRIAGE DRILL PLAN

Title: Quarterly MCI Tabletop Exercise — Multi-Vehicle Collision

DATE & TIME:
- Date: April 15, 2026
- Time: 1:00–4:30 PM (3.5 hours)
- Location: Conference Room B (to avoid ED operations)

OBJECTIVES:
By end of exercise, participants will:
1. Correctly apply START triage protocol to realistic patient scenarios (target: ≥90% accuracy)
2. Demonstrate clear communication under simulated surge conditions
3. Identify and escalate surge protocol activation decisions appropriately
4. Demonstrate resource allocation decisions for 18-patient incident

SCENARIO OVERVIEW:
Incident: Multi-vehicle collision on Highway 101, 1:05 PM
Details:
- 4-vehicle collision at I-70 interchange
- Estimated 15–20 patients, mix of critical and minor injuries
- Fire/EMS on scene, patients transported over 45-minute window
- All patients to City Hospital ED

Incident Timeline:
- 1:05 PM: Incident occurs
- 1:15 PM: Hospital ED notified
- 1:22 PM: First patient arrives (Immediate category patient)
- Subsequent patients arrive in waves (peak at 1:30–1:45 PM)
- Last patient arrives: ~2:05 PM
- Patient list with vital signs and injuries prepared (see Appendix A)

PARTICIPANTS:
- ED Director (facilitator)
- Triage Lead RN (participant)
- ED Nurses (4 participants)
- Physicians (2)
- ED Technicians (2)
- Trauma Surgeon (can attend remotely or designate backup)
- Hospital Operations (1 observer)
- Training Coordinator (observer/evaluator)

EXERCISE FLOW:

1:00–1:05 PM: SETUP & BRIEFING
- Participants gather, introductions
- Scenario overview: Multi-vehicle collision, 1:05 PM, ~18 patients expected
- Objectives: Practice triage, surge activation, communication
- Rules: Realistic but we pause for safety; no pressure; learning focus
- Questions?

1:05–1:15 PM: SCENARIO PRESENTATION
- "You're the ED director. It's 1:15 PM. You receive a call from EMS..."
- Present initial incident information:
  - Fire reports multi-vehicle collision
  - Estimated 4 patients critical, 6–8 serious, 8–10 minor
  - ETA: First patient in 7 minutes
- Ask: "What do you do?"

1:15–1:30 PM: INITIAL TRIAGE SETUP & DECISIONS
- Participants discuss: Activate surge? Set up triage area? Call staff?
- Expected decisions:
  - ✓ Alert ED to incoming MCI
  - ✓ Prepare triage area
  - ✓ Notify trauma surgeon, OR, nursing leadership
  - ✓ Plan Phase 1 surge activation (prepare to escalate)
- Facilitator: "Good. First patient arriving now..."

1:30–2:15 PM: PATIENT TRIAGE (Main Exercise Period)
- Present patients sequentially (every 3–5 minutes)
- For each patient: Provide vital signs, chief complaint, mechanism of injury
- Participants apply START triage: Immediate, Delayed, Minor, Expectant
- Facilitator provides feedback on accuracy
- Injects: "You've triaged 8 patients. You're at 75% ED capacity. Do you escalate surge?"
- Additional injects: 
  - "Transport is slower than expected. More patients still coming."
  - "One monitor stops working. How do you compensate?"
  - "You have 1 Delayed patient whose condition deteriorates to Immediate. How do you reassess?"

Expected Triage Performance:
- Average time per patient: ~2–3 minutes (discussing, presenting)
- Target accuracy: ≥90% (≥17 of 18 correct)

2:15–2:30 PM: RESOURCE & DISPOSITION PLANNING
- Once all patients triaged: "Now what? Where do they go? What are critical next steps?"
- Expected discussion:
  - Surge protocol escalation (phase activated)
  - OR activation for immediate patients
  - ICU bed needs
  - Mutual aid or patient diversion if needed
  - Staffing surge activation

2:30–2:45 PM: EXERCISE DEBRIEF PREPARATION
- Facilitator: "Exercise ends here. Let's pause and debrief."
- Gather evaluation forms from observers
- Note: Specific triage errors, communication gaps, decisions made

2:45–4:30 PM: FACILITATED DEBRIEF (90 minutes)

**WHAT HAPPENED? (15 min)**
- Facilitator: "Let's review what occurred. What happened?"
- Participants share observations: "We triaged 18 patients in 40 minutes. We escalated surge at 75% capacity. We had 2 triage errors."

**SO WHAT? (45 min)**
- Discuss accuracy: 16/18 correct = 89% (target was 90%)
  - Which 2 were wrong? (Patient #7: altered mental status missed; Patient #12: rib fracture severity underestimated)
  - Why? (Rushed assessment; didn't follow START Step 3 completely)
  - Learning: "Remember START Step 3 is critical; must assess mental status in ALL patients"

- Discuss communication: "Was information clear? Any confusion?"
  - "Triage area and treatment area communication good"
  - "Could have clearer communication to OR about trauma cases"
  - Learning: "Establish clear handoff communication between triage and treatment areas"

- Discuss decisions:
  - "When did you escalate surge? Why?"
  - "Was escalation timing appropriate? Too early? Too late?"
  - "Would you make same decision in real incident?"

- Celebrate successes:
  - "Rapid triage setup (5 minutes)"
  - "Clear incident command and communication"
  - "Good resource allocation decisions"
  - "Staff handled stress well"

**NOW WHAT? (30 min)**
- Improvements identified:
  1. Mental status assessment needs reinforcement (START Step 3)
  2. Clearer communication protocol between triage and treatment (develop standardized handoff)
  3. Protocol adjustment: Escalate surge at 70% vs. 75% capacity (earlier activation)

- Action items:
  | Action | Owner | Target Date |
  |--------|-------|-------------|
  | Protocol training: START mental status step | Training Coordinator | 4/22/2026 |
  | Develop triage-to-treatment handoff procedure | ED Director | 4/30/2026 |
  | Update surge protocol: escalation at 70% | ED Director | 4/30/2026 |
  | Next tabletop exercise | Training Coordinator | Q3 (July) |

4:30 PM: EXERCISE CONCLUSION
- Thank all participants
- Distribute feedback forms (optional, for training program improvement)
- Announcement of next exercise date/time
- Informal discussion, networking

SUPPORTING MATERIALS:

Appendix A: Patient Roster (18 Patients)
- Patient #1: 45 y/o driver, RR 8, radial pulse weak, altered mental status → Immediate
- Patient #2: 35 y/o passenger, RR 24, normal perfusion, alert → Delayed
- [etc., 16 more patients with vital signs and presentations]

Appendix B: Facilitator Guide
- Expected triage category for each patient with protocol justification
- Talking points for injects and decision discussion
- Key learning points to reinforce

Appendix C: Observer Evaluation Form
- Triage accuracy checklist
- Communication quality assessment
- Decision-making evaluation
- Comments/observations

Appendix D: Supplies & Equipment Needed
- Scenario cards (printed, one per patient)
- Facilitator guide (printed)
- Flip chart/whiteboard
- Projector/screen (for scenario presentation)
- Evaluation forms (printed)

BUDGET & RESOURCES:
- Facilitator time: ED Director (3 hours planning + 3.5 hours exercise + 1 hour debrief prep) = 7.5 hours
- Participant time: 8–12 people × 3.5 hours = 28–42 staff hours
- Materials: Printing, flip chart paper, markers (~$50)
- Room rental: Included (internal)
- Estimated total cost: ~$300–500 in staff time + materials

COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT:
- If this is competency assessment exercise:
  - Score each participant on triage accuracy, communication, decision-making
  - Document results in individual files
  - Staff scoring <80%: Schedule remedial training within 30 days
  - Staff scoring ≥90%: Verified competent; document in annual assessment

EVALUATION & IMPROVEMENT:
- Compare results to previous tabletop exercises (trend analysis)
- Share lessons learned with all ED staff
- Update protocols/procedures based on identified gaps
- Incorporate improvements into next exercise (Q3)
- Track action item completion (ownership, deadline, status)
```

## Full-Scale Exercise Variant

If you want a **full-scale exercise** instead of tabletop, I would plan:
- Realistic patient flow with trained actors or staff volunteers
- Actual triage area setup with equipment and supplies
- Realistic transport and treatment
- 4–8 hour duration with more complex scenarios
- Higher cost (actor recruitment, equipment, facility setup)
- Greater fidelity and learning impact for team dynamics
