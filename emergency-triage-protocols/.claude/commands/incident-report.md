# /incident-report — After-Action Report Generation

## Purpose
Generate a structured after-action report (AAR) following a mass casualty incident, surge event, or training exercise. Supports quality improvement and lessons learned documentation.

## When to Use
- Immediately after a real MCI or major surge event (within 48 hours)
- Following a significant training exercise (tabletop, full-scale simulation)
- Quarterly or annual program review
- When a triage decision error or near-miss occurs
- For accreditation or compliance documentation

## Duration
30–90 minutes depending on incident complexity

## Input Provided

I'll request the following information:

### 1. Incident Overview
- **Incident date & time**
- **Incident type:** (MCI, surge event, training exercise)
- **Location:** (scene or hospital)
- **Incident classification:** (vehicle crash, building collapse, weather event, disease outbreak, surge event, exercise, etc.)
- **Total patients:** (estimated and actual if known)
- **Reported injuries/illnesses:** (critical, serious, minor, fatalities)

### 2. Facility Response
- **Activation time:** When did your facility recognize incident/surge?
- **Protocols activated:** (surge phases, mutual aid, external resources)
- **Staffing response:** Number and roles of staff deployed
- **Resource utilization:** Equipment, supplies, beds used
- **Duration:** How long was facility in surge/response mode?

### 3. Triage & Patient Flow
- **Total patients triaged:** Broken down by category
  - Immediate (Red): ___
  - Delayed (Yellow): ___
  - Minor (Green): ___
  - Expectant (Black): ___
- **Triage accuracy:** Any significant misclassifications?
- **Average triage time:** Seconds per patient
- **Patient outcomes:** Admitted, discharged, transferred, expired (if available)

### 4. Resource Performance
- **ED surge protocol:** Which phases activated? When?
- **Staffing adequacy:** Was surge staffing sufficient? Shortfalls?
- **Equipment issues:** Any equipment failures or shortages?
- **Supply consumption:** Any critical supply stockouts?
- **Transport capacity:** Met demand or constrained?

### 5. Communication & Coordination
- **Command structure:** Was incident command clear and effective?
- **Communication:** Were messages clear and timely? Any miscommunications?
- **Coordination:** How well did departments work together?
- **External coordination:** Mutual aid, hospital-to-hospital coordination?

### 6. Challenges & Issues Encountered
- **Major difficulties:** (e.g., triage area setup delays, staff unavailability, equipment failure)
- **Near-misses:** Any close calls or safety concerns?
- **Clinical errors:** Any triage misclassifications or treatment delays?
- **Operational issues:** Bottlenecks, communication failures, resource constraints?

### 7. Lessons Learned & Improvements
- **What went well?** (successes, strengths, good decisions)
- **What needs improvement?** (gaps, challenges, barriers)
- **Root causes:** Why did problems occur? (training gaps, process issues, resource constraints, etc.)
- **Specific action items:** What will we change?
- **Ownership:** Who is responsible for each action?
- **Timeline:** When will improvements be implemented?

## Output Format

I'll generate a comprehensive after-action report including:

### Executive Summary
- Incident overview
- Key findings (successes and areas for improvement)
- Recommended actions

### Detailed Findings

**1. Response Performance**
- Activation timeline
- Surge protocol effectiveness
- Staffing surge adequacy
- Resource utilization efficiency

**2. Triage & Patient Care**
- Triage category distribution
- Triage accuracy assessment
- Patient flow analysis
- Any clinical decision issues

**3. Operational Analysis**
- Communication effectiveness
- Coordination between departments
- Equipment and supply availability
- Transport and logistics performance

**4. Strengths Demonstrated**
- Specific examples of excellent performance
- Staff contributions recognized
- Process efficiencies observed

**5. Areas for Improvement**
- Specific gaps identified
- Root cause analysis for major issues
- Recommendations for process changes

**6. Lessons Learned**
- Key takeaways from incident/exercise
- Protocols or procedures to update
- Training needs identified

### Action Plan
| Issue | Root Cause | Recommended Action | Owner | Target Date | Status |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|-------|-------------|--------|
| [Issue] | [Root cause] | [Action to take] | [Person] | [Deadline] | [To do/In progress/Done] |

### Appendices
- Timeline of incident/exercise
- Patient roster and categorization
- Resource utilization summary
- Attendance and participant feedback (if exercise)
- Supporting documents (incident photos, patient logs, etc.)

## Example After-Action Report

```
AFTER-ACTION REPORT (AAR)

INCIDENT: Mass Casualty Incident — Vehicle Collision
Date: March 15, 2026, 2:30 PM
Location: Highway 101, dispatch to City Hospital ED

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
A multi-vehicle collision on Highway 101 resulted in 18 patients transported 
to City Hospital. The ED successfully activated surge protocols, triaged all 
patients within 40 minutes, and distributed resource appropriately. All 
critical injuries were identified and treated. Key success: clear command 
structure, effective triage application. Improvement area: supply chain 
communication delay for additional IV fluids.

INCIDENT DETAILS:
- Fire reports: 4-vehicle collision, approximately 15-20 patients
- Actual transported: 18 patients
- Injuries: 2 critical (Immediate), 8 serious (Delayed), 8 minor (Minor), 0 fatalities
- Time from 911 call to first patient arrival: 22 minutes
- Time from first to last patient arrival: 35 minutes

ACTIVATION & RESPONSE:
- Hospital notified: 2:38 PM (8 minutes before first patient)
- Surge protocol activated: Phase 1 at 2:45 PM (onset), Phase 2 at 3:15 PM
- Staffing surge response: Phase 1 activated, 6 additional nurses called in (arrived by 3:20 PM)
- Triage area: Set up in waiting room, 2 triage nurses + 1 tech
- Treatment areas: 3 Immediate patients to trauma resus, 8 Delayed to ED beds, 8 Minor to fast track

TRIAGE PERFORMANCE:
- Total patients: 18 triaged in 40 minutes (2.2 min/patient average)
- Triage accuracy: 16/18 correct (89%)
- Misclassifications:
  1. Patient #7: Triaged as Delayed, should have been Immediate (altered mental status initially missed)
  2. Patient #12: Triaged as Minor, should have been Delayed (rib fracture pain understated)
- Learning point: Assess mental status in all patients; don't assume stable appearance = low acuity

RESOURCE PERFORMANCE:
- ED surge capacity: Adequate for patient volume and acuity
- Staffing: Surge staffing adequately met demand
- Equipment: No failures; all monitoring equipment functional
- Supplies: Minor delay in securing additional IV fluids (15-minute delay), but no shortage impact
- Transport: One ambulance diverted to regional trauma center (one critically injured patient)

COMMUNICATION & COORDINATION:
- Incident command: Clear, effective command structure
- Radio traffic: Some congestion at peak (3:00–3:15 PM), but manageable
- ED-to-hospital coordination: Good; OR notified of potential surgical cases
- External coordination: Effective communication with EMS, receiving facilities

STRENGTHS OBSERVED:
✓ Rapid incident recognition and activation
✓ Clear triage protocol application (89% accuracy)
✓ Effective resource allocation (surge phases well-calibrated)
✓ Strong leadership and team coordination
✓ Fast triage process without sacrificing accuracy
✓ Good communication despite high-stress environment
✓ Effective use of fast-track area for minor injuries

AREAS FOR IMPROVEMENT:
1. Mental status assessment: Two triage errors related to mental status evaluation
   - Root cause: Rushed assessment, inadequate vital sign integration
   - Action: Retraining on START Step 3 (mental status = critical discriminator)

2. Supply chain communication: 15-minute delay securing additional IV fluids
   - Root cause: Supply chain not aware of surge protocol activation
   - Action: Update surge protocol to include automatic supply chain notification

3. Pediatric assessment: No pediatric patients in this incident, but protocol not reviewed
   - Root cause: JumpSTART protocol awareness unclear for pediatric patients
   - Action: Annual pediatric triage training to ensure readiness

ACTION PLAN:
| Issue | Root Cause | Action | Owner | Target Date | Status |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|--------|
| Mental status misclassifications | Rushed assessment | Retraining on START Step 3; emphasis on mental status as critical finding | ED Director | 4/15/2026 | In progress |
| Supply chain communication delay | Supply chain not alerted to surge | Update surge protocol to alert supply chain; provide contact list | Operations | 4/1/2026 | To do |
| Pediatric protocol clarity | Limited review of JumpSTART | Quarterly pediatric triage review; staff competency assessment | Training Coordinator | Quarterly | To do |

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Conduct focused training session on START mental status assessment (within 2 weeks)
2. Update surge protocol to include supply chain early notification
3. Schedule quarterly pediatric triage competency reviews
4. Consider full-scale exercise (vs. tabletop) to practice patient volume beyond 18
5. Evaluate adding pediatric-focused triage officers to surge team

LESSONS LEARNED:
- Our surge protocols work well at volumes up to ~20 patients
- Triage accuracy is good but mental status assessment needs refinement
- Communication and coordination were strong (no major breakdowns)
- Supply chain integration important for extended surge operations
- Pediatric preparedness should be regularly tested

ACKNOWLEDGMENTS:
- Excellent performance by ED triage team (89% accuracy under pressure)
- Quick surge staffing response
- Effective incident command and leadership

Next AAR review: 6 months (or if another significant incident occurs)
Prepared by: ED Director
Date: March 17, 2026
```

## How to Use This Report

### Immediate Use (Within 48 hours)
- Share findings with ED leadership and clinical team
- Highlight successes and reinforce good practices
- Address critical safety issues immediately (e.g., mental status training)

### Quality Improvement
- Use action items to drive protocol updates
- Assign ownership and track progress
- Schedule follow-up training based on identified gaps

### Accreditation & Compliance
- Submit to hospital quality improvement department
- Document compliance with triage protocols
- Support TJC or CMS compliance reviews

### Continuous Improvement Program
- Log in `work-log/session-log.md` for long-term trend analysis
- Quarterly review of all AARs to identify patterns
- Annual summary of lessons learned across all incidents/exercises

## Report Storage

Save the generated report:
- **Location:** `outputs/` directory with filename: `AAR_[Date]_[IncidentType].pdf` or `.docx`
- **Backup:** Store in facility document repository for compliance retention
- **Sharing:** Distribute to relevant staff, leadership, and quality improvement teams
- **Retention:** Per facility records management policy (typically 5+ years for real incidents)
