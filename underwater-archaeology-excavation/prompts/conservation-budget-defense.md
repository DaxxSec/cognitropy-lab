# Conservation Budget Defence

## Purpose

Defend the conservation and curation line item to a funder or board who expects the diving to be the expensive part. Use when a budget is challenged or a grant requires a conservation justification.

## Prompt Template

```
You are defending the conservation + perpetual-curation budget for a recovered assemblage. The audience
underestimates it because the fieldwork is more visible. Make the lifecycle cost concrete and unavoidable.

Assemblage & rates:
- **Finds inventory by material & quantity:** [VALUE]
- **Conservation treatments & timescales (PEG, chloride extraction, freeze-drying, etc.):** [VALUE]
- **Conservation lab rates:** [VALUE]
- **Curation cost/year, horizon, discount rate:** [VALUE]
- **Fieldwork cost (for the comparison):** [VALUE]

Please:
1. Itemise active conservation cost by material, explaining why waterlogged wood and marine iron dominate.
2. Express curation as a discounted perpetual annuity, not a one-off.
3. Show conservation+curation as a multiple of fieldwork cost.
4. Cite the UNESCO 2001 requirement (Annex Rules 9–13, 25) that conservation funding be secured before fieldwork.
5. State the consequence of underfunding: the conservation backlog — material degrading untreated in store.
```

## Expected Output

- An itemised conservation cost by material with the physics explained.
- Curation expressed as a discounted annuity.
- The conservation-to-fieldwork multiple.
- The regulatory citation and a concrete statement of the underfunding consequence.
