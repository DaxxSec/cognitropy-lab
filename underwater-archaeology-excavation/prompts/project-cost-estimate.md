# Full-Lifecycle Project Cost Estimate

## Purpose

Build a complete, lifecycle cost estimate for a proposed excavation — survey through perpetual curation — so the CBA never stops at the boat. Use when scoping budget or responding to a grant call.

## Prompt Template

```
You are costing a submerged-heritage excavation across its full lifecycle. Carry every phase to present value;
conservation and curation are first-class, not a rounding error.

Site & plan:
- **Site & depth:** [VALUE]
- **Proposed scope (full / sample / rescue):** [VALUE]
- **Required productive bottom-time:** [VALUE]
- **Dive mode & team size:** [VALUE]
- **Proposed finds inventory by material:** [VALUE]

Rates & assumptions:
- **Vessel / team / equipment day-rates:** [VALUE]
- **Conservation rates & timescales:** [VALUE]
- **Curation cost/year & horizon:** [VALUE]
- **Discount rate; weather downtime allowance:** [VALUE]

Please:
1. Estimate each phase: DBA, survey, fieldwork (diver-days → calendar days → cost), conservation, curation, post-ex.
2. Discount future phases to present value.
3. Report conservation+curation as a multiple of fieldwork cost (the headline ratio).
4. Flag any material that available conservation capacity cannot treat.
5. List the two or three cost drivers most worth reducing.
```

## Expected Output

- A phase-by-phase cost table with present values.
- The conservation-to-fieldwork multiple, called out explicitly.
- A capacity-vs-demand flag for untreatable material.
- The top cost drivers and how to reduce them.
