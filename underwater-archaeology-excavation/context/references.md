# Underwater Archaeology Excavation — Reference Tables

Compact lookup data for tasks. Cost figures are **order-of-magnitude planning ballparks** for relative comparison, not quotes — always replace with project-specific rates before presenting a CBA.

## Lifecycle Cost Structure (relative weight)

| Phase | Typical share of lifecycle cost | Cost driver | Notes |
|-------|-------------------------------|-------------|-------|
| Desk-based assessment | Low | Researcher time | Cheapest; do first |
| Geophysical survey | Low–Medium | Vessel + instrument day-rate | Scopes everything else |
| Excavation fieldwork | Medium | Vessel + diver/ROV days | Visible but not dominant |
| **Conservation** | **High (often largest)** | Lab time, years of treatment | The classic under-budgeted item |
| **Curation in perpetuity** | **High (discounted)** | Climate-controlled storage forever | A permanent liability |
| Post-ex analysis & publication | Medium | Specialist time | Often grant-funded separately |

## Diving Access vs. Cost Multiplier

| Mode | Practical depth | Relative cost | When |
|------|----------------|---------------|------|
| SCUBA / scientific air | to ~30–40 m | baseline | Most shallow coastal sites |
| Surface-supplied air | to ~50 m | higher | Longer bottom time, comms, safety |
| Mixed gas (trimix) | to ~100 m | much higher | Technical/deep diving |
| Saturation diving | >100 m | very high | Rare in archaeology; cost-prohibitive |
| ROV | ~no limit | high (vehicle/vessel-bound) | Deep sites; recording + light recovery |
| AUV | ~no limit | high | Autonomous survey/photogrammetry |

## Survey Instrument Selection

| Instrument | Detects | Coverage | Resolution | Best for |
|-----------|---------|----------|------------|----------|
| Side-scan sonar | Exposed relief/structure | High | Moderate | Finding exposed wrecks/mounds |
| Multibeam (MBES) | Bathymetry, 3-D relief | High | High | Scour, exposure quantification |
| Magnetometer/gradiometer | Ferrous mass | Linear/lane | N/A (anomaly) | Buried iron: cannon, anchors, engines |
| Sub-bottom profiler | Buried structure/strata | Linear/lane | Depth-dependent | Buried hulls, stratigraphy |
| Diver/ROV photogrammetry | Visual + 3-D model | Local | Very high | Ground-truth and recording |

## Excavation Method Trade-off

| Method | Cost/speed | Site disturbance | Recovery quality | Use |
|--------|-----------|------------------|------------------|-----|
| Hand-fanning | Slow / low-cost | Lowest | Highest control | Near fragile finds |
| Airlift | Fast / needs compressor | Moderate (if controlled) | Good for bulk overburden | Fine sediment removal |
| Water (induction) dredge | Fast / depth-agnostic | Moderate | Good | Standard sediment removal at depth |
| Grid + spit excavation | Methodical | Controlled | High (stratigraphy preserved) | Systematic stratigraphic dig |

## Conservation Treatment Lookup

| Material | State underwater | Treatment | Rough timescale |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Waterlogged wood | Cell walls degraded | PEG impregnation → controlled / freeze-drying | Months to years (large timbers: decades) |
| Marine iron | Concretion; metal may be void | Chloride extraction (alkaline / electrolytic) | Many months to years |
| Leather / textile / rope | Waterlogged | Bulking + freeze-drying | Months |
| Bone / ivory | Waterlogged, possibly demineralised | Controlled consolidation + drying | Months |
| Copper alloy | Often stable, may concrete | Mechanical/chemical cleaning, stabilisation | Weeks to months |
| Glass / ceramic | Usually stable | Desalination | Weeks |

## UNESCO 2001 Annex Rules — the ones that bite a CBA

| Rule | Substance |
|------|-----------|
| Rule 1 | In situ preservation is the **first option** to be considered. |
| Rule 2 | Commercial exploitation for trade/speculation is fundamentally incompatible with protection. |
| Rule 3 | Excavation must not adversely affect the heritage more than necessary. |
| Rule 4 | Non-destructive techniques preferred over recovery. |
| Rules 9–13 | Project design, funding, and **guaranteed conservation/curation funding before fieldwork**. |
| Rule 25 | Adequate provision for **conservation** for the duration and after. |
| Rule 33 | The project archive must be kept together, intact, and accessible. |

## Significance Assessment Criteria (benefit currency)

Common heritage-significance dimensions (after Burra Charter / national HER frameworks): **research/scientific value, historic value, aesthetic/artistic value, communal/social value, rarity, period representativeness, group/association value, preservation state (potential).** Score each, weight, and aggregate for the benefit side of the ledger.

## Operating Cheat-Sheet

- **Lifecycle ≫ fieldwork.** If conservation+curation aren't on the ledger, the CBA is wrong.
- **In situ is the default;** excavation needs a research-or-threat justification.
- **Discount future costs and benefits;** state the rate; sweep it.
- **Secure conservation capacity before lifting.** Annex Rules 9–13/25 require it; physics enforces it.
- **Significance is the benefit currency** — score it with a rubric, never assert it.
